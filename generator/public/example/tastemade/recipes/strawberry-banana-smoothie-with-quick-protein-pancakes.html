Strawberry Banana Smoothie with Quick Protein Pancakes

Whip up the most important meal of the day with a protein-boosted strawberry banana smoothie and fluffy pancakes.

By Tastemade Culinary Team

Updated: 9/27/2024

Prep Time: 20 min
Cook Time: 25 min
Servings: 6
Easy
Unit conversion unavailable

Ingredients

Strawberry Ginger Smoothie

  • 1 cup sliced frozen strawberries
  • 1/2 ripe frozen banana, peeled
  • ½ tsp ground ginger or 1" peeled ginger knob
  • 1 cup milk, vanilla almond milk or coconut milk
  • 1 scoop Isopure Zero Carb Protein Powder, Unflavored
  • Candied ginger for garnish

Quick Protein Pancakes

  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 1 tbsp apple cider vinegar or lemon juice
  • 1 1/4 cup all purpose flour
  • 1 heaping scoop of Isopure Zero Carb Protein Powder, Unflavored
  • 1 tsp aluminum free baking powder
  • ½ tsp baking soda
  • ½ tsp kosher salt
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 tbsp of melted butter, plus more for pan

Strawberry Ginger Compote

  • 1 pint strawberries, hulled and sliced
  • ¼ cup honey
  • 1-2 inches fresh ginger, grated
  • Kosher salt

Steps

Strawberry Ginger Smoothie

  1. Add all ingredients to a blender and puree until smooth.
  2. Pour into glass and drink immediately.

Quick Protein Pancakes

  1. In a medium bowl or glass measuring cup, add milk. Stir in vinegar and let sit for 5-10 minutes.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together flour, protein powder, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.
  3. To the milk mixture, add egg, vanilla and butter, whisk until fully combined.
  4. Pour into flour mixture and stir until combined.
  5. Do not over-mix, small lumps throughout the batter are fine.
  6. Heat a large skillet or griddle over medium heat.
  7. When hot, add 1 tsp of butter, swirl pan to coat.
  8. Pan should be hot when adding the batter.
  9. Using a 1⁄4 cup measuring cup, scoop batter onto the hot skillet.
  10. Cook pancakes until bubbles form on the top, if the underside is browning too fast, lower heat, about 1 to 2 minutes.
  11. Flip and cook about 1 to 2 minutes longer.
  12. Serve immediately, or place in a 200F oven until ready to serve with honey or maple syrup.
  13. Repeat with remaining batter, using tsp of butter each time to grease the pan.

Strawberry Ginger Compote

  1. In a small saucepan, on medium heat, stir together all ingredients.
  2. Cook until strawberries are still a bit chucky and the sauce is syrupy and thick.
  3. If the sauce is too liquidy, continue cooking on low heat.
  4. Sauce should be a bit spicy from the ginger, but also sweet from the honey.
  5. If using frozen strawberries, cook longer to evaporate the water in the berry, however keep an eye to not burn.
  6. Once the water has evaporated and the sauce is thick, remove from the heat.
  7. Once cool, this compote is ready to use or store in the fridge!
