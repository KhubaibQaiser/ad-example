Classic Gumbo

Chef Alisa Reynolds' famous gumbo is a surefire way to get the party started. This classic southern seafood dish is perfect for a celebration.

By Tastemade Culinary Team

Updated: 10/2/2024

Recipe from:

Make this Tonight

Prep Time: 20 min
Cook Time: 35 min
Servings: 6
Easy
 :

Ingredients

  • 1/4 cup grapeseed oil
  • 1/4 cup gluten free flour, if you like a thicker gumbo
  • 1/2 cup chopped onion
  • 1–2 andouille sausage links, sliced into 1-inch pieces
  • 1/2 cup chopped green red & yellow bell pepper
  • 2 tablespoons chopped celery
  • 1 tablespoon minced garlic 5–6 pieces
  • 1 teaspoon celery seed
  • 2 teaspoons onion garlic paprika mixed together
  • Fresh thyme 3 sprigs
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon salt, to taste
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1 1/4 quarts shrimp or lobster stock or combo of both
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Pickled okra sliced 1/4 inch
  • 1/2 pound shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 1/2 pounds dungeness or king crab legs
  • 1/2 pint scallops medium small
  • 1 pound claw crabmeat, picked over
  • Hot cooked brown rice for serving
  • 2 tablespoons minced fresh parsley
  • 1/4 teaspoon gumbo file

Steps

  1. In a small saute pan, heat 1/2 cup oil. Gradually whisk in the flour, being careful of sputtering oil. Reduce the heat to low and cook and stir the roux continuously until light brown and smooth (think peanut butter colored.) Set aside.
  2. Heat a large pot to medium heat. Add the onion, green onions, bell pepper, celery, andouille sausage and stir until the vegetables are wilted but not browned, about 7 minutes. Add the garlic, celery seed, dry thyme, salt, black pepper, and cayenne. Reduce the heat to low and cook to allow the flavors to blend.
