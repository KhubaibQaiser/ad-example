Food Scrap Hacks

Looking for ways to use your leftover food scraps? Try roasting your potato peels and butternut squash seeds for a delicious, low-waste snack! Don't toss your stale bread — turn that loaf into freezer bread crumbs instead. And if you've got an almost-empty jar of peanut butter, you've got the start to an easy, make-ahead breakfast.

By jen phanomrat

Updated: 10/2/2024

Prep Time: 10 min
Servings: 8
Easy
 :
Nutrition information unavailable

Ingredients

Roasted Potato Peels

  • Russet potato peel
  • Olive oil, to taste
  • Cayenne, to taste
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Roasted Butternut Squash Seeds

  • Butternut squash seed
  • Olive oil, to taste
  • Cumin, to taste
  • Smoked paprika, to taste
  • Cinnamon, to taste
  • Sugar, to taste
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Freezer Bread Crumbs

  • Stale bread

End-of-the-Jar Chia Seed Pudding

  • Almost empty peanut butter or chocolate spread jar
  • 1/4 cup chia seeds
  • 1 cup milk
  • Maple syrup, to taste

Steps

  1. Drizzle potato peels with olive oil and season with cayenne powder, salt and pepper.
  2. Spread evenly on a baking sheet and bake at 400°F for 15–20 minutes, stirring halfway through roasting.
