Corona Extra Poached Hot Dog with Cheesy Sweet Plantains

A play on the Puerto Rico classic appetizer called pionono. It’s savory ground beef topped with mozzarella and rolled into a slice of sweet, ripe yellow plantain.

By monti carlo

Updated: 9/28/2024

Prep Time: 30 min
Cook Time: 1 hr
Servings: 8
Easy
Unit conversion unavailable

Ingredients

  • 1 cup Corona Extra
  • 1/4 cup of high-quality olive oil
  • 1 bay leaf
  • ¼ teaspoon peppercorns
  • ⅛ teaspoon kosher salt
  • 1 red onion, ¼ inch dice
  • 6 cloves garlic, smashed
  • 8 hot dogs
  • Salt, to taste
  • 8 hot dog buns
  • 4 tablespoons salted butter, softened
  • 4 frozen whole yellow plantains, sliced into ¼-inch thick long slices, then defrosted
  • 1 cup mozzarella cheese
  • 2 tablespoons chopped cilantro, for garnish

Steps

  1. Place a rack in the middle of your oven and preheat the oven to 350°F. Line a sheet pan with parchment paper. Set a grill pan over medium heat.
  2. Set a large heavy-bottomed saucepan over medium heat. Add the Corona Extra, oil, bay leaf, peppercorns, salt, onions, and garlic. Bring the mixture to a gentle simmer and add the hot dogs to the pan. Cover the pan and poach the hot dogs for 20 minutes, turning them occasionally to make sure they cook evenly.
  3. When the 20 minutes are up, take the pan off the heat. Drain the liquid out of the pan. Line a plate with paper towels and place the hot dogs on the plate so they can dry. Reserve the chopped onion for garnish.
  4. When the hot dogs have dried off some, add them to the grill pan and cook until they develop some char. Meanwhile, butter the buns, layer each side with a long strip of plantain, and top with cheese. Place them on the sheet pan and then in the oven to toast, face side up, until the cheese is bubbly- about 5 minutes.
  5. Remove the buns from the oven and place the hot dogs on the bun. Garnish with the pickled diced onion and cilantro.
