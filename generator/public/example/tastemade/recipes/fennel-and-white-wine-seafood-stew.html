Fennel and White Wine Seafood Stew

Clams, shrimp, mussels and tuna are simmered in a rich broth of fennel, garlic, leeks and white wine just until tender. Garnished with a fresh dill and fennel salad and served with crusty bread for dipping.

By megan mitchell

Updated: 9/29/2024

Prep Time: 5 min
Cook Time: 20 min
Servings: 6
Easy
Unit conversion unavailable

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp. unsalted butter
  • EVOO, for drizzling
  • 1 leek, cut into 1/2-inch thick half-moons
  • 2 small fennel bulbs, thinly sliced half-moons, fronds reserved
  • 3 garlic cloves, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 tsp. red chili flakes
  • Kosher salt, to taste
  • Freshly cracked black pepper, to taste
  • 1 cup dry white wine, such as Viognier
  • 8 oz. bottle calm juice
  • 6 oz. tuna filet, cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 1/2 pound peeled and deveined shrimp, tails on (21/25 count)
  • 1 pound Manilla clams
  • 1/2 pound mussels
  • 1/4 cup dill fronds
  • 2 tbsp. champagne vinegar
  • Crusty French bread, for serving

Steps

  1. Bring a 12-inch low braiser up to heat over medium.
  2. Add in the butter and 1-2 tbsp. EVOO along with the leek, all but 1/2 cup of the sliced fennel, garlic and chili flakes.
  3. Sauté for 10 minutes until golden brown and softened, liberally season with salt and pepper.
  4. Add in the wine and clam juice, scraping up and brown bits that may have formed on the bottom of the pan.
  5. Next add in the tuna, followed by the shrimp.
  6. Lastly top with the clams and mussels, making sure to nestle everything down into the sauce.
  7. Cover, and simmer for 5-7 minutes or until all of the clams and mussels have opened, the shrimp is pink and the tuna is cooked through.
  8. While the seafood simmers make the topping.
  9. In a small bowl add the reserved 1/2 cup of fennel along with 1/2 cup torn fennel fronds, torn dill fronds, vinegar, 1 tbsp. EVOO and a sprinkle of salt and pepper.
  10. Toss together then sprinkle over the stew right before serving.
  11. Serve with crusty bread for dipping.
