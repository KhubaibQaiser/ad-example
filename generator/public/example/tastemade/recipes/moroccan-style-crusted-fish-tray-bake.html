Moroccan-Style Crusted Fish Tray Bake

Dinner just got a whole lot easier, and tastier, with this Moroccan-inspired tray bake recipe.

By Tastemade Culinary Team

Updated: 10/3/2024

Prep Time: 15 min
Cook Time: 50 min
Servings: 4
Easy
Unit conversion and nutrition information unavailable

Ingredients

Moroccan-Style Crusted Fish Tray Bake

  • Tray Bake: 4 Birds Eye Seeded Crust Fish Fillets with Linseeds and Sunflower Seeds
  • 1.5 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 2 - 3 cloves of garlic, crushed
  • 1 large yellow onion, peeled and cut into 8 wedges
  • ½ of each, medium sized red, green, yellow pepper, cut into rough 1 inch chunks
  • 1 large carrot, peeled and cut into rough 1 inch chunks
  • 1 tin of chickpeas, rinsed and drained
  • 1 heaped tablespoon of Harrisa paste
  • ½ teaspoon paprika powder (optional)
  • ½ teaspoon cumin powder
  • ½ teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 220g cherry tomatoes on the vine, cut into smaller bunches
  • 200ml - 250ml low salt vegetable stock
  • Salt and fresh black pepper to taste

Tahini Sauce

  • 1 clove of garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon of tahini
  • 1 heaped tablespoon of low fat plain yoghurt
  • 1 lemon, juiced
  • Pinch of cumin
  • 1 teaspoon olive oil
  • Pinch of salt
  • Water

To Serve

  • Herby couscous
  • Toasted flaked almonds
  • Handful of fresh parsley and coriander, roughly chopped
  • Drizzle of tahini sauce

Steps

Moroccan-Style Crusted Fish Tray Bake

  1. Preheat the oven to 200°C.
  2. In a deep sided large baking tray, combine the olive oil, garlic, vegetables, chickpeas, Harissa, paprika, cumin and turmeric and mix well to coat all of the ingredients.
  3. Pour in 200ml stock, give it a quick stir and place the vine tomatoes on top and bake in the oven for 25 minutes.
  4. Remove and increase the oven temperature to 220°C.
  5. Give the baked vegetables a gentle stir, taste and check for seasoning. If the stock has been absorbed too much, add a little bit more.
  6. Lay the Birds Eye Seeded Crust Fish Fillets on top, pop the tray back into the oven for another 20 minutes.
  7. Meanwhile, make the tahini sauce.

Tahini Sauce

  1. Whisk together all of the ingredients.
  2. Depending on the thickness of the tahini, add a few small teaspoons of water to loosen. It should have a drizzling consistency.

To Serve

  1. Once cooked, serve the fish and vegetables with some herby couscous.
  2. Spoon over some of the tray juices onto the vegetables.
  3. Sprinkle with the flaked almonds, fresh parsley and coriander, and a drizzle of the tahini sauce and serve.
