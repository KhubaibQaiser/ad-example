Spicy Mediterranean Goat Cheese Balls

This spicy Mediterranean appetizer is perfect with a side of bread for dunking, and packed with flavor thanks to plenty of herbs!

By Tastemade Culinary Team

Updated: 10/2/2024

Prep Time: 30 min
Cook Time: 35 min
Servings: 1
Easy
 :
Nutrition information unavailable

Ingredients

  • 1 generous glug of rape seed oil
  • 1 white onion
  • 3 cloves crushed and roughly chopped garlic
  • 2 red peppers - charred and skins peeled, roughly torn into strips
  • 2 tins cherry tomatoes
  • 1 heaped teaspoon paprika
  • 2 teaspoons chilli flakes
  • 1 glug red wine vinegar
  • 1 handful of fresh oregano and parsley, roughly chopped
  • Generous sprinkle of sea salt flake and freshly ground black pepper
  • 5 1/4 ounces goats cheese - roughly cut into 1-inch squares
  • 5 1/4 ounces feta - roughly cut into 1-inch squares
  • 1 handful of fresh oregano and parsley, roughly chopped
  • 1 generous sprinkle of sea salt flakes and freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 loaf sourdough, sliced, to serve

Steps

  1. Begin by heating 3–5 generous glugs of oil in a large pan or casserole dish over a medium to low heat. Add in the chopped onion and garlic and cook until soft and the onions become translucent, this should take around 6–7 minutes.
  2. Next add in the tin tomatoes (the cherry tinned tomatoes make for a sweeter sauce but regular will work too). Add in the paprika and pepper flakes plus a dash of red wine vinegar and allow to cook for as long as you have time for, minimum thirty minutes. The longer you cook this over a low heat the more delicious and rich the flavors become.
