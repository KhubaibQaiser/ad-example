Cauliflower Shawarma With Mujadara

The veggie feast you never knew you needed!

By Tastemade Culinary Team

Updated: 9/28/2024

Prep Time: 30 min
Cook Time: 45 min
Servings: 4
Easy
 :

Ingredients

Cauliflower Shawarma

  • 1 whole cauliflower, with leaves
  • 2 3/4 ounces tahini
  • Juice of 1/2 lemon
  • 1–2 garlic cloves, crushed
  • Sea salt flake
  • 2 tablespoons pomegranate seeds
  • 3 1/2 ounces unsalted butter, softened to room temperature
  • 1 tablespoon harissa
  • Juice 1 lemon
  • 1 garlic clove, crushed
  • 1 1/2 tablespoons coriander, finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 tablespoon sumac
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons ground cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground allspice

Component 2

  • 8 3/4 fluid ounces sunflower oil
  • 4 medium onions, thinly sliced
  • 8 3/4 ounces brown lentils, cooked
  • 2 teaspoons cumin seeds
  • 1 1/2 tablespoons coriander seeds
  • 7 ounces basmati rice
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons ground allspice
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons ground cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • Salt and black pepper
  • 12 1/4 fluid ounces water

Steps

  1. Trim the outer cauliflower leaves, but leave some of the smaller inner leaves on. Put the cauliflower in a large pot in enough salted water to cover it, and bring to the boil on a high heat. Once the water is boiling, turn the heat down to medium, and cook for approximately 8 minutes. Remove from the water and drain.
  2. Whilst the cauliflower is cooking, whisk the tahini, lemon juice and garlic with 1/2 tablespoon sea salt in a large bowl. Gradually add ice-cold water. At first the tahini will form a lumpy paste, but continue to add liquid and keep whisking until it turns into a smooth, silky sauce and reaches drizzling consistency.
