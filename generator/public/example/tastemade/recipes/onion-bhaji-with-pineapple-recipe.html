Onion Bhaji With Pineapple

Homemade bhaji, a classic Indian fritter, can be made using simple ingredients you likely already have in your pantry and fridge! Whip up a caramelized pineapple chutney for the perfect hit of sourness and acidity in every bite.

By Tastemade Culinary Team

Updated: 9/25/2024

Prep Time: 29 min
Cook Time: 29 min
Servings: 2
Easy
 :
Nutrition information unavailable

Ingredients

  • 3 white onions
  • 2 large eggs
  • 4 1/4 ounces plain flour
  • 1/2 red chilli
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 17 3/4 fluid ounces vegetable oil for frying
  • 8 3/4 fluid ounces natural yogurt
  • 1 lime, juice and zest
  • 1 tin pineapples
  • 4 tablespoons brown demerera sugar
  • 1/2 red chilli
  • 2 tablespoons white/red wine vinegar

Steps

  1. For the chutney, cut the tinned pineapple into rough 3/4 inch chunks. Place the sugar into a heavy based saucepan and bring to a caramel, add in the pineapple chunks and deglaze with the vinegar. Add the chilli and cook for 10–15 minutes until soft. Use a hand whisk to blitz to a rough chutney consistency, using a little of the pineapple juice if too thick.
  2. For the bhajis, mix together the eggs, flours, spices and the sliced chilli to form a paste with a pinch of salt. Slice the onions finely and add to the batter and combine and set aside.
