California Avocado and Citrus Sunshine Salad

This delicious salad is the perfect pairing of rich buttery California Avocados and a mix of bright tart local citrus, topped with fresh herbs, nuts, and a deliciously unique pistachio vinaigrette.

By Tastemade Culinary Team

Updated: 9/28/2024

Prep Time: 20 min
Cook Time: 20 min
Servings: 6
Easy
Unit conversion unavailable

Ingredients

Pistachio Viniagrette

  • ¼ cup pistachios, toasted, divided
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • 1 teaspoon whole grain mustard
  • 1 teaspoon honey, thinned out with a little hot water, if needed
  • 1 teaspoon apple cider vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon shallot, finely chopped
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • Zest and juice of one lemon
  • ½ cup extra virgin olive oil
  • ⅛ teaspoon sea salt, or more to taste
  • ⅛ teaspoon pepper, or more to taste

Salad

  • 2 large, ripe, fresh California Avocados, peeled and sliced
  • 1 blood orange, peeled and sliced into rings
  • 1 cara cara orange, peeled and sliced into rings
  • 1 ruby grapefruit, peeled and sliced
  • 1 oro blanco grapefruit, peeled and sliced
  • 2 kumquats, thinly sliced
  • ¼ cup lightly packed mint leaves
  • ¼ cup lightly packed basil leaves
  • Flaky salt, to finish

Steps

Pistachio Viniagrette

  1. With a mortar and pestle, or with a sharp chef’s knife, crush the pistachios. Reserve. In a medium bowl whisk together mustards, honey, vinegar, shallot, garlic and lemon juice and zest. Stream in olive oil a little at a time until emulsified. Season to taste. Stir in ½ of the crushed pistachios.

Salad

  1. On a platter, arrange your avocado and citrus slices. Tuck in basil and mint leaves evenly. Scatter remaining crushed pistachios. Finish with flaky salt.
  2. Drizzle a few large spoonfuls of pistachio vinaigrette. Reserve the remaining vinaigrette for your guests to add.
  3. Chef’s Tip: Use all the fresh citrus you have available! We are using 2 ½ cups total so whatever you have on hand is perfect.
