Smoked Tea Greens

These smokey, savory collard greens are a celebration of chef and activist's Michael Twitty's personal history.

By Ingrid Nilsen

Updated: 9/27/2024

Recipe from:

Cooking With Pride

Prep Time: 3 hr 50 min
Cook Time: 3 hr 30 min
Servings: 4
Easy
 :
Nutrition information unavailable

Ingredients

Vegetable Stock

  • 1 yellow onion, thinly slice
  • 4 green scallions, white and green parts, thinly sliced
  • 4 garlic cloves, skins removed crushed with the side of a knife blade
  • 1 ounce dried shiitake mushrooms
  • 1 ounce dried porcini mushrooms
  • 2 teaspoons sea salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon coarsely black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon of smoked paprika
  • 4 tablespoons of tomato paste
  • 1/2 pound white button mushrooms, sliced
  • 2 small carrots, roughly chopped
  • 1 parsnip, roughly chopped
  • 1 turnip roughly chopped
  • 6 parsley sprigs, roughly chopped
  • 3 fresh thyme sprigs
  • 2 fresh oregano sprigs
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 slice fresh ginger
  • 3 cups of strong lapsang suchong tea essence (take four bags and let them steep for an hour or more in 3 cups of boiling hot boiling hot water; the essence will develop as the water cools to room temperature)
  • 6 cups of distilled or purified water or spring water

Greens

  • 5 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 1 large red onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 garlic cloves, thinly sliced
  • 1 good pinch of red pepper flakes
  • 1 1/2–2 cups of tea stock
  • 2 pounds of collard greens, washed, stripped from the stem, very thinly sliced into long thin strips
  • 1 red, yellow and orange bell pepper, all seeded and thinly sliced
  • 2 cups of fresh tomatoes, diced
  • Salt, to taste

Steps

  1. Add olive oil to the stockpot.
  2. Add the onion, scallions, garlic, shiitake and porcini mushrooms, and seasonings.
