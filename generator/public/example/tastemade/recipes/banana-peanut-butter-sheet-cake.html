Banana Sheet Cake with Peanut Butter Frosting

For your next celebration, go big with an easy and delicious banana sheet cake smothered in fluffy peanut butter frosting and sprinkled with a flurry of M&M’S® Peanut Butter Minis.

By Tastemade Culinary Team

Updated: 9/29/2024

Prep Time: 25 min
Cook Time: 35 min
Servings: 54
Easy
Unit conversion unavailable

Ingredients

Banana Cake

  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 3½ teaspoons baking powder
  • 2 teaspoons ground cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon sea salt
  • 4 large very ripe bananas
  • 1 cup light brown sugar
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 cup vegetable oil
  • 4 large eggs, room temperature
  • 1 cup buttermilk
  • 1 tablespoon pure vanilla extract

Peanut Butter Frosting

  • 8 ounces cream cheese, room temperature
  • 4 tablespoons unsalted butter, room temperature
  • 1 cup peanut butter
  • 3½ cups powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 2-3 tablespoons milk, if needed
  • ½ cup M&M’S® Minis Peanut Butter

Steps

Banana Cake

  1. Preheat the oven to 350 degrees. Generously spray a 13x18 inch sheet tray with cooking spray.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, cinnamon, baking soda, and salt. Set aside.
  3. In a medium bowl, peel and mash the bananas.
  4. In another large bowl, whisk together sugars and vegetable oil until fully combined. Add eggs and buttermilk and whisk for about 2 minutes until mixture is combined and slightly lighter in color.
  5. Stir in the mashed bananas. Add to the dry ingredients and use a rubber spatula to stir until combined. Do not over mix - only until you don’t see the flour anymore.
  6. Pour batter into the greased sheet tray and level off with a spatula. Place on the middle rack in the oven and bake for 30-35 minutes or until a toothpick inserted in the middle comes out clean. Rotate the sheet tray halfway through for a more even bake. Remove from the oven and allow to completely cool before frosting.

Peanut Butter Frosting

  1. In a stand mixer bowl with a whisk attachment, add cream cheese and butter and whisk on medium speed until combined. Scrape down sides as needed.
  2. Whisk in peanut butter and vanilla until fully mixed. Turn off the mixer, add 2 cups powdered sugar and whisk on slow speed until almost combined, then turn off the mixer and add another 1 ½ cups.
  3. Whisk slowly until all powdered sugar is combined, then turn speed up to fully whip. If the frosting looks dry, add 2-3 tbsp milk to smooth out.
  4. Dollop the frosting onto the cake in sections, then use a spatula to evenly coat. Sprinkle cake with M&M’S® Peanut Butter Minis and enjoy!
