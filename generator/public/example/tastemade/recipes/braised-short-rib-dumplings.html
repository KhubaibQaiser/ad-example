Braised Short Rib Dumplings

Chef Nyesha Arrington celebrates her Korean grandmother's culture and cuisine in these "little pockets of love," which rely on short ribs for a rich, tender filling.

By nyesha arrington

Updated: 9/28/2024

Recipe from:

Behind The Dish

Prep Time: 1 hr
Cook Time: 15 min
Servings: 3
Easy
 :
Nutrition information unavailable

Ingredients

Braised Short Rib Filling

  • 8 pounds trimmed short ribs
  • 1 small sweet onion
  • 1 small pear, peeled and cored
  • 1/3 cup mirin
  • 2 medium carrots, roughly cut
  • 1 onion, cut into 2-inches chunks
  • 1 1/2 cups of soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons of minced carlic
  • 1/2 cup of brown sugar
  • Korean chili flake
  • 1/4 cup sesame seeds, toasted & pulverized
  • 5 cups water
  • 3 tablespoons parsley, chopped
  • 2 teaspoons thyme, chopped

Dumpling Dough

  • 1 pound sifted flour
  • 2 1/2 cups boiling water
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon kosher salt

To Assemble

  • Braised short rib filling
  • Dumpling dough
  • Red mustard frill
  • Beef crackling
  • Beef fat
  • Beef broth

Steps

  1. Place trimmed short ribs in a large pot or Dutch oven. Cover with cold water and bring to a boil. Boil ribs for five minutes, skimming off any residue; then drain the ribs and rinse.
  2. In a food processor, add 1 small sweet onion and one small peeled & cored pear. Pulse until smooth puréed. Scrape down the sides as you go. Scrape the mixture into a bowl. Stir 1/3 cup mirin into the bowl.
