Boozy Cinnabons

Your favorite mall treat is getting a boozy upgrade! Chef Derrell Smith shares his recipe for easy homemade Cinnabons infused with Cognac.

By Tastemade Culinary Team

Updated: 9/26/2024

Prep Time: 30 min
Cook Time: 2 hr 30 min
Servings: 2
Average
 :

Ingredients

Yeast

  • 1/4 ounce active dry yeast
  • 2 teaspoons sugar
  • 1/4 cup warm water
  • 1/4 cup seltzer water
  • 2 teaspoons olive oil
  • 2 teaspoons kosher salt
  • 2 1/4 cups all-purpose flour

Filling

  • 4 ounces butter, softened
  • 1 cup coconut sugar
  • 2 teaspoons ground cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 2 tablespoons cognac

Frosting

  • 1/4 cup softened cream cheese
  • 1/2 cup powdered sugar
  • 1 capful cognac
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream

Steps

  1. In a small bowl or liquid measuring cup, add sugar and yeast into warm 1 teaspoon water. Gently stir with a fork, and set aside for about 5 minutes until foamy and bubbling.
  2. In the bowl of a stand mixer fitted with the dough hook attachment add 1/4 cup flour and seltzer water and a pinch of salt. Mix on low speed. With the mixer running, add the yeast mixture and olive oil. Alternate adding flour and seltzer water little by little until combined. Increase the heat to medium and knead for 8 to 10 minutes until elastic. Remove the dough from the bowl and place in a lightly oiled bowl. Cover with a slightly damp towel and place in a dry, warm place for 2 hours until doubled in size.
