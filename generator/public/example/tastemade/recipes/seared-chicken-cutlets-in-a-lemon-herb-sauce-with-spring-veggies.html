Seared Chicken Cutlets in a Lemon Herb Sauce with Spring Veggies

Chicken breasts are seared, then pulled into large pieces and tossed with roasted heirloom carrots, asparagus, spinach and zucchini drizzled with a lemon, herb sauce.

(1)

By megan mitchell

Updated: 9/29/2024

Prep Time: 10 min
Cook Time: 20 min
Servings: 4
Easy
Unit conversion unavailable

Ingredients

Lemon Herb Sauce

  • 2 lemons, zest and juice
  • 1/3 cup EVOO
  • 1/3 cup dill fronds, roughly chopped (plus more for garnish)
  • 1/3 cup flat leaf parsley, roughly chopped (plus more for garnish)
  • 2 tbsp. minced chives
  • Kosher salt, to taste
  • Freshly cracked black pepper, to taste

Seared Chicken Cutlets w/ Spring Veggies

  • 1 bunch heirloom carrots (not purple), washed and cut into 2-inch pieces on the bias
  • Water
  • 1 bunch asparagus, woody ends trimmed and cut into 2-inch pieces on the bias
  • 1 bunch spinach, trimmed
  • Kosher salt, to taste
  • Freshly cracked black pepper, to taste
  • EVOO, for drizzling
  • Four 3-4oz. boneless, skinless chicken fillets, pounded 1/2-inch thin

Steps

Lemon Herb Sauce

  1. To a small bowl add all of the ingredients for the herb sauce. Mix together.

Seared Chicken Cutlets w/ Spring Veggies

  1. Bring a 12-inch high sided skillet up to heat over medium.
  2. Add in the carrots and enough water to come up halfway.
  3. Cover, and simmer for 10 minutes.
  4. Remove the lid and let most of the water evaporate, about 3-4 minutes.
  5. Next add 1/2 of the herb sauce to the skillet along with the asparagus and spinach, cover and cook for 3 minutes or until the spinach has wilted.
  6. Stir together and season with salt and pepper. Keep warm.
  7. Place the chicken in a large bowl, add the remaining 1/2 of the herb sauce, toss together and let sit while the skillet comes to heat.
  8. Bring a 12-inch skillet up to heat over medium.
  9. Drizzle in 2 tbsp. EVOO then add in the chicken, cooking 2-3 minutes per side or until golden brown and cooked through.
  10. To serve, place a chicken cutlet into a shallow bowl, top with some of the veggies, a sprinkle of fresh herbs and a drizzle of EVOO.
