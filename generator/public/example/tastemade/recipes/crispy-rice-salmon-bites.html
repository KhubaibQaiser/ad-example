Crispy Rice and Salmon Bites

Fresh and packed with flavor, these crispy rice spicy salmon bites make the perfect two-bite appetizer or easy main.

By Tastemade Culinary Team

Updated: 9/28/2024

Prep Time: 30 min
Cook Time: 30 min
Servings: 40
Easy
Unit conversion unavailable

Ingredients

Crispy Rice

  • 1 ½ cups sushi rice
  • 2 cups of water
  • 2 tbsp rice vinegar
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • ½ cup vegetable oil for frying

Salmon

  • 10 oz sushi-grade salmon, finely chopped
  • 4 tbsp mayonnaise
  • 2 tsp ponzu sauce
  • 2 tsp soy sauce
  • 1 green chilli, finely chopped

Avocado

  • 2 small avocados, de-stoned and cut into cubes
  • Juice of a lime
  • Large pinch of salt

Serving

  • 1 Green Chilli, finely sliced
  • Black pepper
Get Ingredients

Steps

Crispy Rice

  1. Wash the rice 4-5 times until the water is clear. Cook the rice as per the package instructions.
  2. Meanwhile, in a small bowl mix the rice vinegar, sugar and sesame oil together until the sugar has dissolved, then set aside.
  3. Once the rice is cooked, toss the mixture into the rice and mix well to combine.
  4. Place your seasoned rice into the square baking trays, lined with plastic wrap, and shape rice into a ½ inch thick square block.
  5. Wrap and refrigerate for at least 1 hour or overnight. To speed up this step, you can place rice into the freezer for 30 - 45 minutes.
  6. When ready to fry the rice, remove the tray from the fridge/freezer and peel from the plastic wrap. Using an oiled knife, cut into bite-sized chunks. Each tray should make about 20 bites.
  7. In a medium skillet, add ½ cup of vegetable oil - or enough oil so the skillet has a thin layer of oil across the whole surface.
  8. Heat the oil on high heat until hot, then gently place the rice into the pan, leaving about 1 inch between pieces.
  9. Turn heat to medium high. Cook rice for about 3 minutes, or until golden brown, then flip and cook for an additional 2-3 minutes.
  10. Remove the rice from the pan and place on a kitchen paper lined baking sheet to drain any excess oil.

Salmon

  1. As the rice is cooling, add all the salmon ingredients into a large bowl and mix to combine.
  2. Taste and season as desired.

Avocado

  1. In a medium bowl, add in all the avocado ingredients and using a fork gently mash to combine until there are no large chunks.
  2. Season with more lime and salt if desired.

Serving

  1. Spoon avocado onto the crispy rice squares followed by a dollop of salmon.
  2. Garnish with a slice of chilli and a grind of pepper.
