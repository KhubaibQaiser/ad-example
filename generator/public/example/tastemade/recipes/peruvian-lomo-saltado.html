Peruvian Lomo Saltado

Experience a taste of Peru with this traditional stir fry that combines seasoned beef, onions, tomatoes and potatoes.

By Tastemade Culinary Team

Updated: 9/28/2024

Prep Time: 1 hr 30 min
Cook Time: 1 hr
Servings: 4
Easy
Unit conversion and nutrition information unavailable

Ingredients

For the Beef

  • 1 ¾ pounds beef tenderloin
  • 1 red onion
  • 1 plum tomato
  • 1 scallion, sliced
  • Salt, to taste
  • Pepper, to taste
  • 1 ½ teaspoons ground cumin
  • 6 ½ tablespoons corn or sunflower oil
  • 1 ½ teaspoons garlic, minced
  • 4 ½ teaspoons ginger, grated
  • 3 ½ tablespoons wine vinegar
  • 7 fluid ounces soy sauce
  • 3 ½ fluid ounces oyster sauce
  • 1 ½ teaspoons oregano
  • Cilantro leaves, to taste

For the Potatoes

  • 1 ¼ pound Peruvian potatoes
  • 33 fluid ounces corn or sunflower oil
  • Salt, to taste

For Serving

  • Cooked beef and vegetable mixture
  • Cooked potato wedges
  • Cilantro leaves, for garnish
  • Cooked long grain rice, optional

Steps

For the Beef

  1. Cut beef into 1.5 inches cubes. Set aside.
  2. Slice the red onion, tomato and scallions into julienne strips. Set aside.
  3. In a bowl, season the beef with salt, pepper and cumin.
  4. In a frying pan or wok over high heat, seal the meat pieces with oil.
  5. Add the minced garlic and grated ginger, sauté quickly.
  6. Add the onions and tomato.
  7. Season with vinegar, soy sauce, oyster sauce and oregano.
  8. Finish with cilantro leaves. Set aside.

For the Potatoes

  1. Cut the potatoes into wedges, leaving the skin on.
  2. Cook them in plenty of oil until they are crispy.
  3. Strain with a slotted spoon and use blotting paper to remove excess oil.
  4. Season with salt and set aside.

For Serving

  1. Place the potatoes on the bottom of the plate.
  2. Serve the meat and the vegetables over the potatoes.
  3. Garnish with cilantro leaves.
  4. Serve with cooked, long grain rice if desired.
