Lumpia Shanghai

Prawns and pork make up the fillings of these crispy lumpia.

By Numra Siddiqui

Updated: 9/27/2024

Recipe from:

If These Meals Could Talk

Prep Time: 2 hr
Cook Time: 10 min
Servings: 4
Average
 :

Ingredients

For the prawn filling

  • 10 1/2 ounces peeled prawns
  • 1 tablespoon carrot, minced
  • 1 tablespoon celery, minced
  • 1 tablespoon water chestnut, minced
  • 1 tablespoon onion, minced
  • 2 teaspoons garlic, minced
  • 2 fluid ounces datu puti cane vinegar (or rice wine vinegar)
  • 2 fluid ounces shaoxing rice wine (or dry sherry)
  • 2 3/4 fluid ounces soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon salt

For the pork filling

  • 10 1/2 ounces minced pork belly
  • 1 tablespoon light soy sauce
  • 1/2 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1/2 tablespoon shaoxing rice wine (or dry sherry) (or dry sherry)
  • 1/2 tablespoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon white pepper
  • 3/4 fluid ounce vegetable oil
  • 3/16 ounce potato starch

For the dipping sauce

  • 3 1/2 fluid ounces datu puti cane vinegar (or rice wine vinegar)
  • 3/4 ounce caster (superfine) sugar
  • 1/4 fluid ounce squid brand fish sauce
  • 3 garlic cloves, peeled and crushed
  • 2 birds eye chillies, chopped
  • 2–2 rounds freshly ground black pepper

For the Lumpia

  • 30 spring roll wrappers
  • 2 large eggs, beaten
  • Oil, for deep frying

Steps

  1. To make the prawn filling, place all the ingredients in a stand mixer fitted with a dough hook and mix for 10 minutes, so the ingredients become well combined but not a purée.
