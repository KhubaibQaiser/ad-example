Chicken Paté with Kumquat Preserves

Chicken livers are sautéed with onions and garlic just until tender then pureed into a silky smooth mouse, spooned into dishes and chilled until ready. Served with a quick-made kumquat jam and toasted bread.

By megan mitchell

Updated: 9/29/2024

Prep Time: 50 min
Cook Time: 30 min
Servings: 6
Average
Unit conversion unavailable

Ingredients

Chicken Pâté

  • EVOO, for drizzling
  • 1 pound organic, free-range chicken livers
  • 5 green onions, thinly sliced
  • 2 garlic cloves, smashed
  • 2 bay leaves
  • Kosher salt, to taste
  • Freshly cracked black pepper, to taste
  • 2 tbsp. brandy
  • 10 tbsp. unsalted butter (divided)
  • 3-4 sage leaves
  • Toasted bread or crackers for serving

Kumquat Preserves

  • 16 oz Kumquats
  • 1 small Meyer lemon
  • 6 tbsp. granulated sugar

Steps

Chicken Pâté

  1. Bring a 10-inch high sided skillet up to heat over medium.
  2. Drizzle in 2 tbsp. EVOO then add in the livers, green onions, garlic and bay leaves.
  3. Sauté until the livers are cooked through, about 10-12 minutes.
  4. Season with salt and pepper then deglaze the pan with brandy, cook until absorbed, about 1 minute.
  5. Remove from heat and let cool slightly.
  6. Remove the bay leaves then pour mixture into a food processor and blitz until a coarse chop. With the machine running add in 6 tbsp. of butter, 1 tbsp. at a time. Puree until smooth and creamy, scraping down the sides as needed. Taste for seasoning, adding more salt or pepper as needed.
  7. Spoon the mixture into a shallow dish then cover with plastic wrap directly touching the pâté.
  8. Place in the fridge to chill fully, at least 30 minutes.
  9. While the pâté chills make the preserves. (see kumquat preserves recipe)
  10. To a small sauce pot add the remaining 4 tbsp. of butter. Bring up to heat over medium. When bubbling add in the sage leaves and fry for 1 minute.
  11. Remove the leaves then pour the browned sage butter over the pâté. Place back in the fridge until the butter has solidified.
  12. When ready to serve, top with the fried sage leaves and serve with warm jam and toasted bread.

Kumquat Preserves

  1. Thinly slice the kumpats, discarding all the seeds and place in a small sauce pot.
  2. Add the zest and juice from the lemon along with the sugar. Stir.
  3. Bring up to heat over medium low and simmer, until the fruit has broken down and a thick jam remains, about 15 minutes, stirring often.
