Pistachio Molten Lava Cakes

This molten lava cake has a quick prep & cook, while highlighting flavors of pistachio and white chocolate. Serve with your favorite ice cream!

By Joe Sasto

Updated: 9/29/2024

Prep Time: 15 min
Cook Time: 12 min
Servings: 4
Easy
Unit conversion unavailable

Ingredients

  • ½ cup unsalted butter
  • 4 oz white chocolate, chopped
  • ⅓ cup pistachio paste (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1 large eggs
  • 4 large egg yolks
  • ⅓ cup granulated sugar
  • ¼ cup all-purpose flour
  • ¼ teaspoon salt
  • ¼ cup finely chopped pistachios (optional, for texture)
  • Powdered sugar (for dusting)
  • Ice cream (for serving)

Steps

  1. 1. Preheat the oven: Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). Grease four 6-ounce ramekins with butter and lightly dust them with flour. Shake out the excess.
  2. 2. Melt the butter and white chocolate: In a microwave-safe bowl, melt the butter and white chocolate together in 20-second intervals, stirring between each, until smooth and fully melted. Set aside to cool slightly.
  3. 3. Prepare the batter: In a nutribullet SmartSense blender pitcher, blend together the eggs, egg yolks, melted butter, white chocolate, and pistachio on setting 1 for 15 seconds and increase to setting 2 for 10 seconds.
  4. 4. Add the dry ingredients: Gently fold in the flour and salt until just combined. Be careful not to over mix. If desired, fold in the finely chopped pistachios for extra texture.
  5. 5. Fill the ramekins: Divide the batter evenly among the prepared ramekins, filling each about ¾ full. Place the ramekins on a baking sheet and bake in the preheated oven for 10-12 minutes, or until the edges are set but the centers are still soft and jiggly.
  6. 6. Cool and serve: Let the cakes cool in the ramekins for 1-2 minutes. Carefully run a knife around the edges to loosen the cakes and invert each ramekin onto a serving plate. Dust the tops with powdered sugar and serve immediately with a scoop of ice cream.
