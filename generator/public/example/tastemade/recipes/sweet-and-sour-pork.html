Healthy Sweet and Sour Pork

Enjoy a lighter twist on a classic Chinese takeout dish: sweet and sour pork! Chef Erwan Heussaff harnesses all the flavors you love with a few minor tweaks, for a satisfying dish you'll feel good about eating.

By Erwan Heussaff

Updated: 9/26/2024

Recipe from:

Lightened Up

Prep Time: 15 min
Cook Time: 22 min
Servings: 2
 :

Ingredients

Sweet and Sour Pork

  • 1/4 cup tapioca starch
  • 2 large eggs, beaten
  • 1 cup almond flour
  • Canola oil, as needed
  • 1 pound pork tenderloin, cut into 3/4-inch pieces
  • 2 tablespoons minced garlic
  • 1/2 red bell pepper, cut into 3/4-inch pieces
  • 1/2 green bell pepper, cut into 3/4-inch pieces
  • 1/2 large yellow onion, cut into 3/4-inch pieces
  • 1/2 cup fresh pineapple chunks
  • Sesame seed, for garnish

Sauce

  • 1/2 cup fresh pineapple juice
  • 1/4 cup rice vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 1 1/2 tablespoons tapioca starch
  • 1/2 tablespoon tamari or soy sauce

Steps

  1. Set up a dredging station with the tapioca starch, eggs and almond flour. Carefully dredge each piece of pork in the tapioca starch, then the egg and then the almond flour, until completely coated. Shake off any excess almond flour.
  2. Heat 2 tablespoons of oil in a large saucepan on medium-high heat, add the pork and cook until cooked through and golden brown. Set aside.
