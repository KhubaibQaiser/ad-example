Pork Sausage Bao

Artist and activist, Erin O’Brien, grinds up some homemade sausages and chats about using food as performance art. She and Ingrid stuff up her Pork My Buns XXXX to honor her friend Chris’s progressive work as a trans filmmaker.

By Ingrid Nilsen

Updated: 10/2/2024

Recipe from:

Cooking With Pride

Prep Time: 45 min
Cook Time: 30 min
Servings: 2
Easy
 :
Nutrition information unavailable

Ingredients

Seasoning

  • 1/2 cup honey granules
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce or soy sauce powder, if available (powder is ideal because when it comes to sausages you want less liquids)
  • 1/2 cup hoisin sauce
  • 1/4 huangjiu if you can get it but any mijiu or shaoxing work
  • 1/4 fermented soybeans (tương hột or salted fermented soy bean not the japanese natto)
  • 2 tablespoons fermented red bean curd
  • 3 tablespoons 5 spice powder
  • 2 tablespoons red yeast sea salt (you can also use regular diamond coarse salt if you can’t find red yeast salt)
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon ginger powder

Pork Sausage

  • 10 pounds boned out or boneless pork shoulder
  • 2–2 1/2 pounds pork fatback
  • Pork casing 1 inch

Bao

  • Fresh made sausage
  • Steamed bao bun (the folded kind)
  • Hoisin sauce
  • Green onion
  • Firm cucumber
  • Cilantro leaf

Steps

  1. Mix seasoning ingredients together and blend with stick blender.
