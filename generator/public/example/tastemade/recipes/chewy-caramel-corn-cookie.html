Caramel Corn Chewy Cookie

This sweet and chewy cookie recipe will satisfy your dessert cravings with a mix of texture and flavor, thanks to gooey caramely and crunchy corn flakes.

By Tastemade Culinary Team

Updated: 10/5/2024

Prep Time: 45 min
Cook Time: 30 min
Servings: 12
Easy
Unit conversion unavailable

Ingredients

Caramel Kellogg’s Corn Flakes® topping

  • 3 cups Kellogg’s Corn Flakes®
  • ½ stick Unsalted butter
  • 3 tablespoons Wildflower honey
  • Pinch of Kosher salt

Cookie Dough

  • 2 sticks Unsalted butter, cubed
  • 3 tablespoons Wildflower honey
  • ¾ cup Granulated sugar
  • ¾ cup Dark brown sugar
  • 2 ½ cups All purpose flour
  • 1 cup Kellogg’s Corn Flakes®, crushed
  • 1 teaspoon Kosher salt
  • 1 teaspoon Baking soda
  • 2 large Eggs
  • 1 teaspoon Vanilla extract
  • 250g (1 ½ cups) White chocolate, roughly chopped (optional)

Steps

Caramel Kellogg’s Corn Flakes® topping

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F. Measure the Kellogg’s Corn Flakes® into a heat-proof bowl.
  2. Bring the butter, honey, and salt to a simmer and pour over the Kellogg’s Corn Flakes®s.
  3. Use a heat proof spatula to toss the cereal until coated in honey butter.
  4. Spread the coated Kellogg’s Corn Flakes® on a parchment-lined baking sheet and bake for 8 minutes until fragrant and golden, and set the candied cereal aside to cool.

Cookie Dough

  1. While the Caramel Kellogg’s Corn Flakes® topping cools, make the cookie dough.
  2. Melt half a stick of butter over medium heat and bring it to a simmer. Continue cooking until nutty smelling and browned. Add the honey and remove from the heat. Stir until the honey has melted into the browned butter.
  3. Combine the remaining butter, melted honey butter, and both sugars in the bowl of a stand mixer fitted with a paddle attachment. Mix on low speed until creamy and emulsified.
  4. Whisk the eggs and vanilla together. With the mixer running on low speed, slowly add the eggs to the buttery mixture.
  5. Once all the egg has been incorporated, add the crushed Kellogg’s Corn Flakes®, flour, baking soda, and salt.
  6. Mix on low speed until all the flour has been incorporated. If using the white chocolate, add it to the dough and briefly mix on low speed to disperse the chocolate.
  7. If you turned your oven off, now is the time to turn it back on and preheat to 350°F.
  8. Portion the dough into balls (a 2-oz ice cream scoop works great for this) and chill for at least 30 minutes.
  9. Place the balls of dough 3 inches apart on a parchment-lined baking sheet, top each cookie dough ball with a generous spoonful of Caramel Kellogg’s Corn Flakes® topping, and press the candied cereal into the top of the cookie dough ball so it sticks in place.
  10. Bake for 7 minutes. Rotate the baking sheet then gently but confidently tap the baking sheet on a heat-proof surface, like the top of the oven range.
  11. Return the cookies to the oven and continue baking for another 8-10 minutes until risen and browned at the edges but soft and a bit wet-looking in the center.
  12. Remove the cookies from the oven and do another confident tap to settle the cookies before leaving them to cool for 15 minutes before eating warm.
  13. They are best eaten the same day, but if kept in a sealed food storage container will be good up to two days later.
