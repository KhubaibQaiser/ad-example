Corona Extra Battered Hot Dog with Mango Salsa

Inspired by the bacalaito, a Puerto Rican favorite of salt cod fried in a batter, this recipe includes a beef hot dog fried in an adobo-seasoned rice flour batter, placed in a toasted bun, and garnished with mango salsa and cilantro.

By monti carlo

Updated: 10/2/2024

Prep Time: 30 min
Cook Time: 45 min
Servings: 8
Average
Unit conversion unavailable

Ingredients

  • 8 hot dogs
  • Oil, for frying
  • 8 hot dog buns
  • 4 tablespoons salted butter, softened
  • 1 1/2 cup rice flour plus ½ cup more for dredging
  • ½ cup cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon adobo
  • 1 teaspoon sazón
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 12 oz Corona
  • 1/4 cup mayo
  • 2 tablespoons ketchup
  • 1 teaspoon hot sauce
  • 1 cup mango salsa
  • Cilantro, for garnish

Steps

  1. Prep all ingredients first. Place a rack in the center of your oven and preheat the oven to 350°F. Line a sheet pan with parchment paper and a rack. Dry the hot dogs with a paper towel and place them on the rack. Keep them in the oven while it preheats until their casing has dried, about 5 minutes.
  2. Meanwhile, add 4 inches of oil to a heavy-bottomed pot large enough to hold 4 hot dogs without crowding them, like a Dutch oven. Set the pot over medium heat until the oil reaches 375°F. Butter the buns.
  3. Next, make the batter. Add the rice flour, cornstarch, adobo, sazón, baking powder, and baking soda to a bowl large enough to hold a hot dog and mix well. Add the beer and mix until a batter forms. Prepare a rimmed plate, large enough to hold four hot dogs, and add the reserved ½ cup rice flour for dredging.
  4. Take the hot dogs out of the oven, take them off the rack, and place them in the rice flour. Roll them in it to cover them completely. Clean and dry the rack and set it over a few paper towels.
  5. When the oil has reached 375°F, batter the hot dogs. Work in batches of 4 so as not to crowd the pot. First, take a hot dog, shake off excess flour, and then spear the end of the hot dog with a fork. Dip the hot dog in the batter, and shake off the excess. Place the dog in the oil and use a second fork to take it off the first fork. Repeat 3 more times. As the hot dogs fry, turn them to ensure they cook evenly.
  6. Meanwhile, place the buns face-side up on the sheet pan. Place the sheet pan in the oven and remove it when the buns are toasted- about 2 minutes.
  7. When the hot dogs are golden, remove them from the oil and place them on the clean rack to dry. Then place them on a bun and sprinkle them with mango salsa. Garnish with cilantro.
