Seared Salmon with Wilted Spinach and Wild Mushrooms

Seared salmon is served on top of wilted spinach and wild mushrooms then topped with an olive, caper, herb salsa.

By megan mitchell

Updated: 9/29/2024

Prep Time: 30 min
Cook Time: 35 min
Servings: 2
Easy
Unit conversion unavailable

Ingredients

Herb Salsa

  • 2 green onions, roughly chopped
  • 1 garlic clove, peeled
  • 1/2 cup pitted castelvetrano olives
  • 1/4 cup capers
  • 1/4 cup flat leaf parsley
  • 2 tbsp. oregano leaves
  • 2 tbsp. champagne vinegar
  • 2 tbsp. EVOO
  • 1 tbsp. water
  • Freshly cracked black pepper, to taste

Seared Salmon w/ Wilted Spinach and Wild Mushrooms

  • Ghee, for searing
  • EVOO, for drizzling
  • 6 oz. shiitake mushrooms, stems trimmed and torn into pieces
  • 4 oz. oyster mushrooms, torn into pieces
  • Kosher salt, to taste
  • Freshly cracked black pepper
  • 3/4 cup heavy cream
  • Freshly grated nutmeg, to taste
  • 1 bunch spinach, stems trimmed and roughly chopped
  • Two 7oz. wild caught salmon fillets, skin on

Steps

Herb Salsa

  1. To a small food processor add all of the ingredients and blitz until a chunky sauce forms.
  2. Taste for seasoning then pour into a bowl and set aside.

Seared Salmon w/ Wilted Spinach and Wild Mushrooms

  1. Next, bring a 10-inch cast iron skillet up to heat over medium.
  2. Add in 2 tbsp. ghee along with 1-2 tbsp. EVOO.
  3. Once melted add in the mushrooms and let sit, undisturbed for 2-3 minutes.
  4. Stir then let sit again to form a crust.
  5. Once the mushrooms have turned golden brown, about 10-12 minutes, liberally season with salt and pepper.
  6. Turn heat to low and add in the cream and nutmeg.
  7. Stir, taste for seasoning then add in the spinach and cook until wilted, and the cream has reduced, about 8 minutes.
  8. Season again with salt and pepper then keep warm while you cook the fish.
  9. Bring a 10-inch nonstick skillet up to heat over medium.
  10. Liberally season the salmon fillets with salt.
  11. Add 2 tbsp. ghee to the hot skillet then place the salmon, flesh side down, into the skillet.
  12. Cook 3-4 minutes then flip and cook 2-3 minutes more or until your preferred doneness.
  13. To serve, divide the mushroom and spinach mixture between two shallow bowls then top with the salmon fillets and lastly a spoonful of the herb salsa. Serve immediately.
