Melvin Mallory’s Famous Fried Hot Chicken Sando with Southern Coleslaw

Your next game-day party or weekend barbecue calls for this extra-special fried chicken sandwich. Plenty of seasonings make the chicken shine, and a juicy Southern-style coleslaw balances the heat.

By Logan Sandoval

Updated: 10/2/2024

Recipe from:

Make this Tonight

Prep Time: 20 min
Cook Time: 15 min
Servings: 2
Average
 :
Nutrition information unavailable

Ingredients

Fried Hot Chicken

  • 2 boneless skinless chicken thighs
  • Chicken brine
  • Seasoned flour
  • Frying oil
  • Furikake bun
  • Butter, for toasting buns
  • Japanese mayo
  • 1/2 ounce cayenne
  • 1/2 ounce togerashi + extra for garnish

Chicken Brine

  • 3 cups water
  • 3/4 cup kosher salt
  • 3/4 cup sugar
  • 3 bay leaves
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 2 anise pods
  • 1/2 tablespoon coriander seeds
  • 1/2 tablespoon black peppercorns
  • 1/2 tablespoon mustard seeds
  • 1/4 teaspoon allspice berries

Seasoned Flour

  • 2 cups ap flour
  • 1/2 tablespoon lemon pepper
  • 1/2 tablespoon paprika
  • 1/2 tablespoon seasoned salt
  • 1/2 tablespoon white pepper
  • 1 tablespoon cayenne pepper
  • 1 tablespoon kosher salt

Furikake Buns

  • 1 cup lukewarm water
  • 2 tablespoons butter, room temperature
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 large egg, separated
  • 3 1/2 cups ap flour
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1 1/4 teaspoons kosher salt
  • 1 tablespoon instant yeast
  • 5 tablespoons furikake

Southern Coleslaw

  • 1/2 cup japanese mayo
  • 1/3 cup brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup whole milk
  • 1/4 cup buttermilk
  • 2 tablespoons lime juice
  • 2 tablespoons rice wine vinegar
  • 1/2 teaspoon kosher salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper

French Fries

  • 1/3 cup sugar
  • 2 cups warm water
  • 3 russet potatoes
  • 6 cups beef tallow
  • Kosher salt, to taste
  • Black pepper, to taste

Fried Garlic Aioli

  • 15 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 cup japanese mayonnaise
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 3/4 teaspoon kosher salt
  • 1 tablespoon parsley, minced

Steps

  1. Place chicken thighs in brine, cover with plastic wrap, and let brine in the refrigerator for 24 - 48 hrs.
  2. Heat oil over medium to medium-high heat in a deep pot or cast iron skillet to 350°F.
