California Avocado Gremolata Tri-Tip Steak

Taste Tri-Tip in a whole new way thanks to bold California Avocado gremolata. In season California Avocados make this herb, citrus and garlic packed sauce an unforgettable showstopper.

By Tastemade Culinary Team

Updated: 9/28/2024

Prep Time: 1 hr 30 min
Cook Time: 20 min
Servings: 6
Easy
Unit conversion unavailable

Ingredients

Avocado Gremolata

  • 2 large, ripe, fresh California Avocados
  • 1 cup lightly packed parsley leaves, finely chopped
  • ½ cup lightly packed cilantro leaves, finely chopped
  • Zest and juice of one lemon
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • ⅓ cup extra virgin olive oil
  • ⅛ tsp sea salt, or to taste
  • ⅛ tsp freshly ground black pepper, or to taste

Steak

  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 ½ teaspoons granulated garlic
  • 1 tablespoon kosher salt
  • 2 teaspoons freshly ground black pepper
  • 4 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil, divided
  • 1 tri-tip steak, trimmed, about 2 pounds

Steps

  1. In a small bowl, combine rub ingredients.
  2. Coat the tri-tip in 2 tablespoons of olive oil and then season the tri-tip with the rub. Allow to sit and come to temperature for one hour. While the tri-tip marinates, prepare your gremolata. In a medium bowl, combine all ingredients except the avocados. Just before serving peel, seed, and dice the avocados then fold them into the gremolata mixture.
  3. Preheat oven to 400 F. In a 14" cast iron skillet over medium high heat, add remaining 2 tablespoons olive oil until almost smoking. Sear the seasoned tri-tip about 3 minutes on each side. Move the skillet into the oven and finish cooking for about 15-20 minutes, or until the internal temperature reaches 127 F for medium rare or 130 F for medium. Remove skillet from oven and allow to rest for 10-15 minutes before slicing.
  4. Slice against the grain and arrange on a platter. Serve with gremolata on top or on the side.
  5. Chef’s Tip: Tri-tip is a larger tough cut of meat. If you can let the rub penetrate the meat longer, up to 8 hours in the refrigerator, please do! If your tri-tip is untrimmed, remove any silver skin but feel free to keep a thin layer of fat. Score the fat before adding rub and omit rubbing the tri tip with oil. Tri-tip has two grain directions so you will need to slice across the grain carefully and switch directions when needed.
