Turkey and White Bean Stew with Kale and Potatoes

This hearty dish is even better the next day and is a great way to stretch a pound of meat.

By megan mitchell

Updated: 9/29/2024

Prep Time: 20 min
Cook Time: 40 min
Servings: 6
Easy
Unit conversion unavailable

Ingredients

  • EVOO, for drizzling
  • 1 pound dark meat ground turkey
  • Kosher salt, to taste
  • Freshly cracked black pepper, to taste
  • 2 garlic cloves, finely minced or grated
  • 1 tablespoon minced rosemary
  • 1/2 cup Holland House White Cooking Wine
  • 2 1/2 cups low sodium chicken stock
  • 1 tablespoon dijon
  • 16-ounce can cannellini beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 pound baby Yukon gold potatoes, cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 1 bunch lacinato kale, washed, ribs removed and roughly chopped

Steps

  1. Bring a 12-inch high sided skillet up to heat over medium. Drizzle in 2-3 tablespoons of EVOO then add in the ground turkey. Cook until golden brown, breaking up into smaller pieces.
  2. Liberally season with salt and pepper, then add in the garlic and rosemary, cook for 1-2 minutes or until fragrant. Deglaze the pan with Holland House White Cooking Wine, scraping up any brown bits that may have formed on the bottom of the pan.
  3. Reduce the heat to low then add in the stock, dijon, cannellini beans, potatoes and kale. Stir together then season again with salt and pepper. Simmer, covered, on low for 15-20 minutes or until the potatoes are tender. Stir occasionally. If the liquid reduces too quickly you can add a little more stock or water as needed.
  4. Taste for seasoning, adding more salt or pepper as needed. Serve with crusty bread.
