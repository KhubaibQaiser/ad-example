Vegan Haitian "Beef" Kabobs

Not only is this Haitian recipe easy to create, it's also perfect for your upcoming BBQ. To get the meaty mushrooms & vibrant vegetables extra juicy, Chef Gabrielle uses a quick & easy vegan Haitian epis - sinking flavor deep into each & every bite.

By Tastemade Culinary Team

Updated: 10/2/2024

Prep Time: 15 min
Cook Time: 5 hr
Servings: 2
Easy
Unit conversion and nutrition information unavailable

Ingredients

Overall Ingredients

  • 2 - 4 cups oyster mushrooms OR cubed portobello mushrooms
  • 3 cups green pepper (chopped)
  • 3 cups red bell pepper (chopped)
  • 3 - 4 cups chopped white onion
  • 4 - 5 cups of beefy mushroom marinade (see below)
  • Haitian Epis (for serving, see below)
  • Rice and Beans (for serving, see below)

Beef Mushroom Marinade

  • 4 - 5 cups vegetable broth
  • 2 vegan beef bouillon cubes
  • 3 tbsp maple syrup
  • 3 tbsp vegan Worcestershire sauce
  • 2 tbsp garlic powder

Haitian Epis

  • 1 cup fresh parsley
  • 1/2 cup white onion
  • 1 cup red or green bell pepper
  • 2 stems celery (chopped)
  • 6 sprigs fresh thyme
  • 4 cloves garlic
  • 3 tbsp lime juice
  • 2 tbsp apple cider vinegar
  • 1 tsp ground clove
  • 1 tbsp maple syrup
  • 1 vegan bouillon cube
  • 1 cup green onions
  • salt and pepper (to taste)
  • 1 scotch bonnet pepper (optional)
  • 1/3 - 1 cup grapeseed oil
  • 1 cup cooked chickpeas or plain hummus (optional)

Haitian Rice and Beans

  • 1 1/2 cup veggie broth
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 2 tbsp minced garlic
  • 1 can kidney beans (drained)
  • 1 vegan bouillon cube
  • 1/3 cup haitian epis
  • 1/2 tbsp black pepper
  • 1 - 2 tbsp pink himalayan salt
  • 2 cups organic white rice

Steps

Overall Ingredients

  1. Combine all ingredients for the beefy mushroom marinade. Cut the mushrooms into bite-sized cubes and add them to the marinade, allowing them to marinate for at least 3 hours.
  2. Make the Haitian Epis by combining all of the ingredients in a food processor or blender and blending until all of the ingredients are fully combined. Slowly add more oil as you blend to make it smooth and creamy.
  3. Make the Haitian Rice and Beans by adding all of the ingredients into a large pot. Mix well, cover, and cook on medium high for 20-30 minutes.
  4. On a large wooden skewer, pierce the cubed and marinated mushrooms, green and red bell peppers, and white onions. Make sure to spread out the mushrooms and bell peppers making sure that there is a rainbow of colors and flavors on each skewer.
  5. Paint on the Haitian Epis to the vegetable skewers, making sure every single piece has a bit of the seasoning on it.
  6. Bake the seasoned vegetable skewers for 15-25 minutes, painting on more of the Haitian Epis halfway through baking. Bake until the mushroom is juicy and the bell peppers are soft.
  7. To make a creamy dipping sauce add 1 cup plant based mayonnaise or plain hummus to some Haitian Epis and mix it together until well combined.
  8. Serve the spicy Haitian beef kebabs on top of a bed of Haitian rice and beans with the creamy epis dipping sauce on the side.
  9. Sing your song. Do your dance. Speak your truth, and enjoy the Haitian Skewers!
