Healthy Pad Thai

Skip the delivery and whip up a lighter version of pad Thai with Erwan Heussaff's recipe, which relies on sweet potato noodles and plenty of veggies.

By Erwan Heussaff

Updated: 9/27/2024

Recipe from:

Lightened Up

Prep Time: 10 min
Cook Time: 25 min
Servings: 4
Average
 :

Ingredients

Light Pad Thai

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil, divided
  • 12 ounces sweet potato noodles
  • 1 pound chicken breast, cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 8 shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 2 cups carrot, sliced thin
  • 1 cup bean sprouts
  • 1/2 cup red onion, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon garlic, minced
  • 1 egg

Garnish

  • 1/4 cup scallions, sliced
  • 1/3 cup peanuts, chopped
  • 2 limes, cut into wedges
  • 1 red chili, sliced
  • 6 sprigs cilantro, picked

Sauce

  • 1 tablespoon tamarind concentrate
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 1 teaspoon fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon garlic, minced

Steps

  1. Heat 1 tablespoon oil in a large pan on medium-high heat. Cook chicken and shrimp until almost finished then remove from pan and set aside.
  2. Add remaining 1 tablespoon of oil, then onions, garlic and sweet potato noodles. Saute for about 3 minutes. Add half the sauce and stir until noodles have absorbed some of the liquid. Add chicken and shrimp and remaining Pad Thai ingredients and cook through. Move all contents in the pan to the side and scramble the egg in the space made. Once eggs are cooked mix everything together and adjust sauce as needed. Remove from pan, garnish and serve.
