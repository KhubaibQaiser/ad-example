Keftedes - Greek-Cypriot Pork Meatballs

These Greek meatballs feature potato, onion, and juicy minced pork.

By Numra Siddiqui

Updated: 9/29/2024

Recipe from:

If These Meals Could Talk

Prep Time: 1 hr
Cook Time: 15 min
Servings: 4
Average
 :

Ingredients

Keftedes

  • 2 large potatoes
  • 1 onion, peeled and finely chopped
  • 1/2 bunch flat leaf parsley, finely chopped
  • 17 3/4 ounces pork mince (ground pork)
  • 1 large egg
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried mint
  • 1/4 teaspoon white pepper
  • Sea salt
  • 3 1/2 ounces fresh breadcrumbs
  • 2 slices white bread
  • 35 1/4 fluid ounces vegetable oil, for deep frying

Yoghurt Dip

  • 10 1/4 fluid ounces greek yoghurt
  • 1/2 lemon, juice only
  • 1 small bunch mint, leaves picked and chiffonaded
  • 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • To serve: lemon wedge, sliced, tomato, sliced, mint leaf, to garnish

Steps

  1. Peel the potatoes and coarsely grate them into a colander over a large mixing bowl. Squeeze the grated potatoes to get rid of the liquid and starch, then leave to one side while you get the rest of the ingredients ready, reserving the liquid in the bowl underneath.
  2. Mix the onion, parsley, mince (ground pork), egg, baking powder, cinnamon, mint, white pepper and a generous few pinches of salt in a large bowl. Squeeze small handfuls of the grated potato, to really extract all the moisture, and add to the pork mixture. Keep going until it is all used.
