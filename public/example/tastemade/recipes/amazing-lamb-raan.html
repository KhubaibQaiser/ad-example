Lamb Raan

This Lamb Raan is the perfect Diwali showstopper! Served with Indian spiced roast potatoes and gravy, it's the ultimate celebratory feast.

By Tastemade Culinary Team

Updated: 10/2/2024

Prep Time: 12 hr
Cook Time: 4 hr 30 min
Servings: 6
Easy
 :

Ingredients

Mandy's Mum's Garam Masala

  • 1 tablespoon cumin seeds
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 4 black peppercorns
  • 1 tablespoon coriander seeds
  • 1 dry bay leaf
  • 1 black cardamom pod
  • 2 green cardamom pods

Marinade

  • 10 1/4 fluid ounces thick greek yoghurt
  • 1 heaped tablespoon garlic, minced
  • 1 heaping tablespoon ginger, minced
  • 1 heaping tablespoon garam masala powder
  • 1 1/2 tablespoons kashmiri mirch
  • 1/2 tablespoon chilli flakes
  • 1/2 tablespoon cumin powder
  • 1/2 tablespoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 tablespoon salt
  • 3 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons white vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon tomato purée
  • 1 3/4 ounces fried onions
  • 2 tablespoons mustard oil
  • 88 1/4 ounces leg of lamb
  • Baby/micro coriander, pomegranate

Indian Roast Potatoes

  • 88 1/4 ounces maris piper potatoes, peeled
  • 6 tablespoons rapeseed oil, divided, plus extra to drizzle
  • 1/2 tablespoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2–1 teaspoon red chilli powder (as per taste)
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • Coriander and fresh green chillies, for garnish

Steps

  1. Heat up a heavy based frying pan and place the whole spices.
  2. Roast the spices, stirring often until fragrant and lightly toasted. Tip into a coffee grinder and blend until you have a smooth powder.
