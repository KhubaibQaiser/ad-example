Thai Style Whole Fried Fish

Here's the easy way to fry a whole fish for a perfect result!

By Tastemade Culinary Team

Updated: 10/2/2024

Prep Time: 25 min
Cook Time: 15 min
Servings: 2
Easy
Â :

Ingredients

  • 2 sea breams, gutted and scaled, head and tail left on
  • 4 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 4 tablespoons sugar
  • Ginger, spring onion, garlic
  • 3 1/2 ounces rice flour
  • 1 small handful of coriander stalks, roughly chopped
  • 3 long red chillies, de-seeded and roughly chopped
  • 1 tablespoon crushed garlic
  • 1 tablespoon grated ginger
  • 3 shallots, peeled and roughly chopped
  • 1 lemongrass stalk, bashed and sliced
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 3 1/2 fluid ounces water
  • 1 tablespoon tamarind paste
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 2 long red chillis, de-seeded and sliced
  • 3 spring onions, sliced
  • 1 green mango, peeled and thinly sliced
  • 1 carrot, ribboned
  • 1/2 cucumber, ribboned
  • 1 3/4 ounces crispy onions
  • 1 small bunch coriander leaves
  • 1 small bunch vietnamese mint leaves
  • 1 small handful of peanuts or cashews
  • Oil to deep fry
  • Fresh lime wedge, to serve

Steps

  1. Put the fish in a shallow dish and sprinkle the soy and sugar over. Stuff the cavity with some ginger, sliced spring onions and a few bashed garlic cloves, then cover and refrigerate for 30 minutes.
  2. To make the sauce, put the coriander roots, chillies, garlic, ginger, shallot, lemongrass and oil into a spice blender and blend to a paste.
