Poutine

A homemade version of the French Canadian favorite, with crispy potato wedges smothered in gravy and savory cheese curds

By Frankie Celenza

Updated: 9/28/2024

Recipe from:

Struggle Meals

Prep Time: 15 min
Cook Time: 50 min
Servings: 4
Average
 :

Ingredients

Fries

  • 2 pounds russet potatoes
  • 3 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 teaspoons kosher salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper

Gravy

  • 3 tablespoons butter
  • 1 tablespoon onion powder
  • 2 teaspoons garlic powder
  • 3 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 4 cups beef broth
  • 2 teaspoons thyme leaves
  • 1 teaspoon apple cider vinegar
  • Salt and pepper
  • 8 ounces cheddar cheese curds
  • 2 tablespoons minced parsley leaves (optional)

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 450°F. Scrub the potatoes clean. Cut each potato in half lengthwise. Then cut each half into 4 wedges. In a large bowl, toss the potatoes with the vegetable oil and salt and pepper.
  2. Divide the potato wedges between 2 parchment lined sheet trays, laying each wedge flat. Bake for 25 minutes, until they are dark golden brown on the bottom. Flip the potatoes and bake for an additional 20 minutes.
