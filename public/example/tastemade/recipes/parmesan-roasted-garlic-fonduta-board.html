Parmesan & Roasted Garlic Fonduta Board

A variety of cherry tomatoes, baby carrots, cucumber sticks, radishes, blanched asparagus tips, and pasta chips pair excellently with this parmesan & roasted garlic fondue.

By Joe Sasto

Updated: 10/2/2024

Prep Time: 20 min
Cook Time: 30 min
Servings: 8
Easy
Unit conversion and nutrition information unavailable

Ingredients

Fondue

  • 1 head of garlic
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 4 oz. Pecorino Romano, broken into large chunks
  • 4 oz. Parmesan cheese, broken into large chunks
  • 2 cups hot half-and-half
  • Freshly ground black pepper (to taste)
  • Fresh chive/basil oil (optional, for garnish)

Accompaniments

  • Rustic bread, sliced.
  • Pasta chips
  • Cherry tomatoes
  • Baby carrots
  • Cucumber sticks
  • Radishes
  • Blanched asparagus tips

Steps

Fondue

  1. 1. Roast the Garlic: Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C). Slice the top off the garlic head, drizzle with olive oil, and wrap it in foil. Roast for 30 minutes until soft and caramelized. Allow it to cool slightly, then squeeze out the roasted garlic cloves and set aside.
  2. 2. Make the Fondue: Heat the half-and-half in a small saucepan until hot but not boiling. In a nutribullet SmartSense blender pitcher, add the Parmesan cheese, Pecorino Romano cheese, roasted garlic, and hot half-and-half. Blend at setting 1 for 10 seconds then blend on speed 5 for 30 seconds until smooth. If needed, add more half-and-half to reach your desired consistency. Season with freshly ground black pepper to taste.
  3. 3. Serve the Fondue: Pour the fondue into a warmed serving dish or fondue pot. Garnish with fresh thyme and a grind of black pepper. Arrange the toasted bread slices and crudités around the fondue.

Accompaniments

  1. Serve alongside the dip!
