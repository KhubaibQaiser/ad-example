Garlic Sourdough California Avocado Toast

Uplevel the currently trending roasted garlic and shallot butter toast by combining three crave-able Californian heavy hitters: fresh California Avocados, bold garlic, and crusty sourdough.

By Tastemade Culinary Team

Updated: 9/28/2024

Prep Time: 1 hr 10 min
Cook Time: 10 min
Servings: 2
Easy
Unit conversion unavailable

Ingredients

Toast

  • 1 large, ripe, fresh California Avocado, peeled and sliced
  • 2 slices sourdough bread, from the middle of the loaf if round
  • ¼ cup pine nuts, toasted
  • 1 tablespoon mixed fresh herbs (chives, dill, parsley)
  • ½ Fresno chili, finely diced

Shallot Garlic Butter

  • 3 shallots, peeled
  • 3 bulbs garlic, tops sliced off
  • 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil, divided
  • ⅛ teaspoon sea salt, or more to taste
  • ⅛ teaspoon freshly ground black pepper, or more to taste
  • 1 stick unsalted butter, softened
  • ½ cup Parmesan, finely grated
  • ½ teaspoon red pepper flakes
  • Zest and juice of one lemon

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 375 F. Drizzle 2 teaspoons of olive oil over shallots and season with salt. Wrap in aluminum foil. Repeat with garlic cloves in a separate aluminum foil packet. Place on a baking sheet and roast in the oven for 1 hour. When cool enough to handle, chop shallots and squeeze out roasted garlic.
  2. In a medium bowl, combine softened butter with remaining ingredients and shallot/garlic mixture. Spread a thick layer on each slice of bread. Keep any remaining butter in an airtight container for up to 1 week.
  3. Place sourdough slices on a baking sheet and bake for 12-15 minutes, until slightly golden and bread is crisp. When slightly cooled, arrange avocado slices on top and garnish with herbs, pine nuts and chili.
