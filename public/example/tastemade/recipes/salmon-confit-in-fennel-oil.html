Salmon Confit in Fennel Oil

Chef Daisy Ryan's elegant salmon recipe is sure to impress on your next date night. Confit the salmon in a homemade fennel oil and drizzle in a creamy Béarnaise sauce for a beautiful, French-inspired main course.

By Daisy Ryan

Updated: 9/28/2024

Recipe from:

Make this Tonight

Prep Time: 20 min
Cook Time: 1 hr
Servings: 4

Ingredients

Salmon Confit

  • 1 pound salmon (with high fat content, ie. ora king), center cut
  • 1/4 cup kosher salt + 1 quart water (optional brine for salmon)
  • Fennel oil
  • Béarnaise sauce
  • 1/2 cup fresh tarragon leaves, for garnish

Fennel Oil

  • 70 3/4 fluid ounces grapeseed oil
  • 2 whole fennel bulbs, with fronds

Bearnaise Sauce

  • 1 pint clarified butter
  • 3 egg yolks (free-range high quality egg)
  • 1 shallot, grated
  • 1 tablespoon dijon mustard
  • 1 tablespoon champagne vinegar
  • 1/4 teaspoon sriracha (or other hot sauce)
  • 1 tablespoon fine sea salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon fresh ground black pepper
  • 1/2 cup fresh tarragon, chopped

Steps

  1. Portion salmon in 6–8 ounce pieces, or leave it whole if you have a large enough vessel to cook it in.
  2. You can brine your salmon in a saltwater solution and it will add more flavor (1/4 cup of kosher salt to about 1 quart of water and make enough to submerge your fish for about 10 minutes).
