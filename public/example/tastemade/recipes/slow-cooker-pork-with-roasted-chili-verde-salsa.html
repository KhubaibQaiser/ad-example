Slow-Cooker Pork With Roasted Chili Verde Salsa

If you like carnitas, you'll love this delicious, flavorful pork dish.

By Tastemade Culinary Team

Updated: 9/25/2024

Prep Time: 2 hr 20 min
Cook Time: 2 hr
Servings: 4
Average
Unit conversion unavailable

Ingredients

Slow-Cooker Pork With Roasted Chili Verde Salsa

  • 2 pounds pork shoulder, cut into 2-inch cubes
  • Canola oil

Salsa Verde

  • 1 pound fresh tomatillos, husked and rinsed so they aren't sticky anymore (if you can't find fresh, canned will work, too)
  • 1 yellow onion, peeled and quartered
  • 10 garlic cloves, smashed
  • 1 tablespoon freshly ground cumin
  • 1-2 jalapeños (depending on how spicy you like it)
  • 1 bunch of cilantro
  • 4 limes, zested and juiced
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • A glug of olive oil

Steps

Slow-Cooker Pork With Roasted Chili Verde Salsa

  1. Roast vegetables for salsa verde (see recipe below).
  2. Heat a large cast-iron or heavy-duty sauté pan over high heat. Season pork with salt and pepper and rub with a tablespoon or two of canola oil (enough to glisten). Sear pork on all sides, making sure it's golden brown on all sides.
  3. Place pork inside your slow cooker.
  4. Once veggies are done roasting, pour into a blender or food processor along with the lime juice and zest, and blend on high until smooth, adding a touch of water if needed. Dump salsa on top of pork, put the lid on and cook pork until tender, about 4-5 hours on low or 3 hours on high.
  5. Serve with a rice, beans and tortillas.

Salsa Verde

  1. Preheat oven to 450°F.
  2. Toss everything for the salsa verde (except the cilantro and lime juice/zest) on a baking sheet and roast until golden, slightly charred and tender. (If you're using canned tomatillos, skip this step and throw them directly into blender.)
