Green Laksa

Whip up a bowl of laksa, a spicy Southeast Asian noodle soup, right at home. This version uses green curry paste and plenty of aromatics for a bright twist on the classic coconut broth.

By Tastemade Culinary Team

Updated: 10/2/2024

Prep Time: 20 min
Cook Time: 40 min
Servings: 2
Average
 :
Nutrition information unavailable

Ingredients

Green Laksa Paste

  • 3–5 green birds eye chilis, stalks removed
  • 100 grams small shallots, peeled
  • 8 cloves garlic, peeled
  • 30 grams fresh ginger, peeled and roughly chopped
  • 30 grams cilantro stalks
  • 4 stalks lemongrass, white part only, sliced
  • 2 1/2 grams grated fresh turmeric
  • 7 grams coriander powder
  • 6 grams cumin powder
  • 60 grams store-bought laksa paste
  • 22 milliliters store-bought green curry paste

Soup

  • 30 milliliters canola oil
  • 90 grams knorr ginataang gulay
  • 1 1/2 liters boiling water
  • 300 grams rice noodles (vermicelli or wide flat)
  • 150 grams bean sprouts
  • 500 grams shrimp, cleaned and peeled, with tails on
  • 20 packets fish or squid balls
  • 200 grams firm tofu, cubed and fried
  • 2–3 hard boiled eggs, halved
  • Cilantro leaf, for garnish
  • 2 limes, cut into wedges

Steps

  1. Using a pestle and mortar, or in the bowl of a food processor, grind the chilis, shallots, garlic, ginger, cilantro stalks, lemongrass and fresh turmeric with a pestle and mortar until you have a thick paste. Stir in the coriander and cumin.
