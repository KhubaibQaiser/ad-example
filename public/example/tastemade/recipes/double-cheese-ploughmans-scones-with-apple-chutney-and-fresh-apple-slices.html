Double Cheese Ploughman's Scones

You have to try these cheddar cheese scones with apple chutney and fresh apple slices.

By Tastemade Culinary Team

Updated: 10/16/2024

Prep Time: 30 min
Cook Time: 1 hr
Servings: 6
Easy
Unit conversion unavailable

Ingredients

Scones

  • 225g self-raising flour
  • ¼ tsp of cayenne pepper
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • ½ tsp sea salt
  • 55g cold unsalted butter, cut into 1cm cubes
  • 125g Castello Tickler Extra Mature Cheddar, finely grated
  • 50g walnuts, chopped
  • 150ml whole milk
  • 1 large egg

Chutney

  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 small white onion, finely chopped
  • 2cm piece of ginger, peeled & finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, peeled & finely chopped
  • 2 red apples, Pink lady or Braeburn, peeled, cored & chopped into 1cm cubed pieces
  • 50g sultanas
  • 3 tbsp dark brown sugar
  • 7 tbsp red wine vinegar
  • 1 tbsp tomato paste
  • ½ tsp ground cinnamon
  • ¼ tsp chilli flakes
  • 1 tbsp salted butter
  • 150g Castello Tickler Extra Mature Cheddar

Steps

Scones

  1. Preheat the oven to 220C /425F.
  2. Sieve the flour, baking powder, salt and cayenne pepper into a large bowl.
  3. Add the cold cubed butter. Using your fingertips, rub the mixture together until it resembles fine breadcrumbs.
  4. Add the grated Tickler Extra Mature and walnuts, combine well.
  5. Next, crack an egg into a jug, add the milk and whisk well. Make a well in the middle of the flour mixture, and gradually add the milk until the dough comes together (you may not need all of the liquid).
  6. Next, transfer onto a lightly floured surface, and bring the dough back on itself a few times, then flatten and roll out to 3cm thick. Using a floured 5cm round cutter, cut out scones.
  7. Transfer the scones to a lined baking sheet. Brush with the remaining milk and egg mix and sprinkle over the remaining cheese. Place into the preheated oven, on the middle shelf, and bake for 11- 13 minutes until risen and golden brown.
  8. Make the chutney: In a saucepan over a low to medium heat, add the olive oil and onions. Sweat for 5 minutes until soft. Next, add the garlic and ginger and cook for 2 minutes. Add the tomato paste, apples, sultanas, dark brown sugar, red wine vinegar, cinnamon, and chilli flakes to the saucepan. Reduce the heat to low, and simmer for 30 minutes. Watch that the chutney doesn’t catch, and if it gets too dry, add a splash of water. After 30 minutes, using a fork, gently mash the apples briefly. Remove from the heat, and decant into a sterilised jar.
  9. To serve: Using a serrated knife, split the scones and generously slather each side with butter. Place 1 tbsp of chutney onto the bottom scone, and top with a few slices of apple, and finally some shards of Tickler Extra Mature.

Chutney

  1. In a saucepan over a low to medium heat, add the olive oil and onions. Sweat for 5 minutes until soft.
  2. Next, add the garlic and ginger and cook for 2 minutes.
  3. Add the tomato paste, apples, sultanas, dark brown sugar, red wine vinegar, cinnamon, and chili flakes to the saucepan. Reduce the heat to low, and simmer for 30 minutes.
  4. Watch that the chutney doesn’t catch, and if it gets too dry, add a splash of water.
  5. After 30 minutes, using a fork, gently mash the apples briefly. Remove from the heat, and decant into a sterilized jar.
  6. Using a serrated knife, split the scones and generously slather each side with butter.
  7. Place 1 tbsp of chutney onto the bottom scone, and top with a few slices of apple, and finally some shards of Tickler Extra Mature.
