Best Ever Spotted Dick

This simple dessert is light, fluffy, and delicious. And it MUST be served with custard. But we'll let you in on a secret: if you cant find suet, you can use butter instead.

By Tastemade Culinary Team

Updated: 9/28/2024

Prep Time: 30 min
Cook Time: 1 hr
Servings: 6
Average
 :

Ingredients

  • 150 grams dried currants
  • 250 milliliters water
  • 250 grams self raising flour
  • 80 grams caster sugar
  • 7/9 gram salt
  • 100 grams shredded suet
  • 1 lemon, zest only
  • 200 milliliters whole milk
  • Butter, for greasing the dish

Steps

  1. Put the currants into a saucepan with 250 milliliters of water and simmer for 5 minutes, then leave to rest in the water while you prepare the rest.
  2. Generously butter the inside of the pudding dish and prepare the lid by laying a piece of foil on top of a piece of baking paper. Fold the two in half, then leave a 3 centimeters seam and fold them back on themselves. With the foil on top, cut out a circle large enough for the top of the pudding bowl with a 5 centimeters edge all of the way around.
