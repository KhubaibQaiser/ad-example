Layered Ahi Poke with Avocado Salsa

This ahi poke makes the perfect appetizer or light meal.

By Tastemade Culinary Team

Updated: 10/2/2024

Prep Time: 20 min
Cook Time: 5 min
Servings: 2
Easy
 :
Nutrition information unavailable

Ingredients

Layered Ahi Poke

  • 10 wonton wrappers
  • Canola oil for deep frying
  • 2 teaspoons of hawaiian sea salt
  • 1 1/2 pounds sashimi-grade ahi tuna, cubed in 1/4 inch
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 2 teaspoons of toasted sesame seeds
  • 2 scallions thinly sliced
  • 1 tablespoon low sodium soy sauce
  • 1 small serrano pepper, seeded and finely chopped
  • 2 large ripe avocados
  • 2 tablespoons fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 1 inch ginger, peeled and grated
  • 1 lime, juice
  • 1 teaspoon chili sauce
  • 1 teaspoon hawaiian salt
  • 1/2 cup mayonnaise
  • 1 teaspoon dijon mustard
  • Juice from 1/2 lemon
  • 1 tablespoon siracha

Avocado Salsa

  • Scoop avocado into a bowl.
  • Add chili sauce, ginger, lime juice, cilantro, 1 teaspoon salt and mix.
  • Gently mash together. set aside.
  • In a small bowl, add mayo, mustard, lemon, and siracha and mix well.
  • To assemble, on a plate, add a layer of avocado mixture, top with marinated ahi tuna.
  • Drizzle with sriracha mayo and top with wonton strip.
  • Serve immediately or keep in refrigerator until ready to serve.

Steps

  1. Stack wonton wrappers on top of each other and slice in 1/4 inch strips.
  2. Fill a 12 inches skillet with about 1 inch of oil. On high heat, heat oil until hot.
