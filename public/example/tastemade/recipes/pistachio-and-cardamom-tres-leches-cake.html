Cardamom Tres Leche Cake

Pistachio and cardamom lend some South Asian spice to the traditional Mexican tres leches cake, served with dulce de leche ice cream.

By Tastemade Culinary Team

Updated: 9/27/2024

Prep Time: 30 min
Cook Time: 30 min
Servings: 12
Average
Unit conversion unavailable

Ingredients

Cake

  • 2 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tsp baking powder
  • 6 medium eggs
  • 1 ¼ cups granulated sugar
  • 1 ½ tsp vanilla extract
  • A pinch of salt
  • 1 cup evaporated milk

Agave Milk

  • 1 cup agave syrup
  • 1 ⅓ cups milk
  • 1 cup evaporated milk
  • 1 cup condensed milk
  • 10 cardamom pods, bruised

To Serve

  • ½ cup heavy cream, whipped
  • ½ cup pistachios, chopped

Steps

Cake

  1. Heat the oven to 350F. Oil and line a 8 x 12 inch baking dish. Mix the flour and baking powder in a bowl, set aside. In a separate bowl, use and electric whisk to mix the eggs and sugar and until thick and the whisk leaves a trail when it comes out (around 5 minutes).
  2. Sift the flour mixture into the eggs and sugar, fold together. Add the milk and vanilla, fold gently. Pour into the baking dish and bake for 30 mins or until an inserted skewer comes out clean. Cool for 10 minutes, then run a knife around the edges to loosen the sponge from the sides. Poke holes all over with a skewer.

Agave Milk

  1. Add all the ingredients for the agave milk to a small pan, set over a low heat for 5 minutes, mixing well. Leave aside to infuse.
  2. Use a serving spoon to drizzle the agave mixture over the cake. Work gradually, don’t flood it.

To Serve

  1. Top with the whipped cream and pistachios, slice and serve with dulce de leche ice cream.
