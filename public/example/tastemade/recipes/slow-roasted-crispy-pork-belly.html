Slow Roasted Crispy Pork Belly with Drunken Soju BBQ Sauce

Chef Logan Sandoval brings the flavors of Hawaii home with his slow-roasted crispy pork belly drizzled with a "drunken" soju BBQ sauce and served with shiso leaves for easy eating.

By Tastemade Culinary Team

Updated: 10/2/2024

Cook Time: 1 hr 30 min
Servings: 4

Ingredients

Slow Roasted Crispy Pork Belly

  • 2 pounds pork belly skin on
  • 1 1/2 tablespoons shaoxing wine
  • 1 teaspoon chinese five spice powder
  • 3/4 teaspoon kosher salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon coarse ground black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon datu puti spiced vinegar (can substitute with unseasoned rice wine vinegar)
  • 7 ounces rock salt
  • 12 ohba shiso leaf (perilla leaf)s

Drunken Soju BBQ Sauce

  • 3 cups shoyu
  • 3 cups brown sugar
  • 1 cup soju
  • 1/2 cup gochujang
  • 1/2 cup honey
  • 1/3 cup rice wine vinegar
  • 15 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1/4 cup ginger (minced)
  • 4 chopped scallions
  • 1 asian pear
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon korean chili pepper

Steps

  1. Using a sharp carving fork, pierce as many holes in the skin as possible, avoiding piercing the fat and make sure you do not pierce the flesh.
  2. Turn pork belly upside down. Rub flesh with cooking wine. Sprinkle over five spice powder, salt and pepper. Rub all over flesh, including sides (not on skin).
