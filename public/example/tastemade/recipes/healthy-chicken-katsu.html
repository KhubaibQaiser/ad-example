Healthy Chicken Katsu

Chicken katsu is classic Japanese fare traditionally coated in panko breadcrumbs and deep fried. Chef Erwan Heussaff gives the dish a healthy spin by adding in almond meal, and baking the chicken katsu instead of frying it.

By Erwan Heussaff

Ingredients

Healthy Chicken Katsu

  • 2 chicken breasts
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 cup plus 2 tablespoons almond meal
  • 4 tablespoons panko breadcrumbs
  • 2 large eggs, beaten
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

dipping sauce

  • 2 tablespoons ground black sesame seeds
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 5 teaspoons worcestershire sauce
  • 2 teaspoons soy sauce
  • 2 teaspoons honey

garnish

  • 1 cup cabbage, shredded

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 400Â°F.
  2. In a medium-sized saucepan, heat the olive oil over medium-low heat. Add 1 cup of almond meal and panko and toast until fragrant and it has developed a golden-brown color.
