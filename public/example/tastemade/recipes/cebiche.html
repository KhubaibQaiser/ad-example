Peruvian Cebiche

Experience a taste of Peru with this fresh and flavorful cebiche.

By Tastemade Culinary Team

Updated: 9/28/2024

Prep Time: 20 min
Cook Time: 15 min
Servings: 5
Easy
Unit conversion unavailable

Ingredients

  • 2 ¼ pounds Sole fillet
  • 1 red onion
  • Cilantro leaves, to taste
  • 1 teaspoon garlic, minced
  • 4 ½ teaspoons ginger, grated
  • 10 ounces lime juice
  • Salt, to taste
  • Pepper, to taste
  • 5 lettuce leaves
  • 7 ounces sliced sweet potato, boiled, for garnish (optional)
  • 3 ½ ounces corn nuts, for garnish (optional)
  • ¾ ounces sliced rocoto or jalapeño pepper, for garnish
  • Ice, as needed

Steps

  1. Clean the fish fillet of bones and remains of skin and tissue.
  2. Cut into cubes of approximately 1 inch. Set aside in the fridge.
  3. Cut the onion into julienne strips.
  4. Wash the julienned onion with plenty of cold water, dry and set aside.
  5. Wash and remove the cilantro leaves. Set aside.
  6. In a large bowl, add the cut fish, the red onion and the cilantro leaves.
  7. Season with the minced garlic and grated ginger.
  8. Add the lime juice and two or three ice cubes to keep the preparation very cold.
  9. Refrigerate the mixture for 10-15 minutes.
  10. Season to taste with salt and pepper.
  11. On a plate, serve the cebiche on lettuce leaves with slices of boiled sweet potato, corn nuts, cilantro and pepper slices.
