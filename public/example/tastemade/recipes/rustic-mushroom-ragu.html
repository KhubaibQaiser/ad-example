Rustic Mushroom Ragu

This will become your favorite way to make mushroom pasta. It's rustic, earthy, acidic, robust and comes together fairly quickly! You can also make the sauce in advance and leave it on the stove for later.

By Noah Galuten

Updated: 9/28/2024

Recipe from:

Don't Panic Pantry

Prep Time: 10 min
Cook Time: 30 min
Servings: 4
Easy
 :
Nutrition information unavailable

Ingredients

  • 1 pound of any any mushrooms, or even an assortment (white button, cremini, shiitake, maitake, bunashimeji, oyster, etc.)
  • 5 medium shallots, or 2–3 large shallots, diced (or just a big onion)
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 3–4 garlic cloves, chopped
  • 1 pinch of crushed red pepper
  • 1–2 sprigs of rosemary
  • 2–3 sprigs of thyme
  • Some kitchen twine (optional)
  • 1/2 cup or so glug of white wine
  • 28 ounces whole, peeled tomatoes, crushed by hand
  • 1 pound of medium medium pasta, like fusilli, rigatoni, etc.
  • Pecorino (or parmesan) closer (optional)
  • Olive oil to finish

Steps

  1. Prep your mushrooms into big, rustic hunks. You can quarter them, rip them, slice them -- the goal is big hunks that you can spear with a noodle.
  2. Add the oil to a dutch oven or large skillet with a lid, over medium heat. Once the oil is shimmering, add the shallots and garlic and season them with salt.
