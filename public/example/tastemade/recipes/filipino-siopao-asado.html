FIlipino Siopao Asado

Siopao Asado, or Filipino steamed buns, are a delicious snack or appetizer. This easy recipe uses pork belly for a savory filling that nobody can resist!

By jen phanomrat

Updated: 9/29/2024

Prep Time: 10 min
Cook Time: 8 min
Servings: 5
Easy
 :

Ingredients

Dough

  • 2 1/2 cups flour
  • 2 teaspoons yeast
  • 2 tablespoons granulated sugar
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon kosher salt
  • 1 cup warm water
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable shortening
  • Salted duck egg, peeled and quartered (optional)

Filling

  • 1 pound pork butt or belly, small cubed
  • 1/2 cup red onion, chopped
  • 4 garlic cloves, chopped
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1/4 cup oyster sauce
  • 1/3 cup soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch + 1/4 cup water
  • Oil for the pan

Steps

  1. In a bowl, combine flour, yeast, sugar, baking powder, and salt. Gradually add warm water while mixing. When a rough dough forms, add shortening and use your hands to knead. Transfer the dough to a lightly floured surface and knead until smooth. Place in an oiled bowl, cover with plastic wrap, and allow to rise for 2 hours or until doubled in size.
  2. Once the dough has risen, transfer to a lightly floured surface, and gently knead to form a log. Divide into 8 equal portions. Gently roll each portion into a ball and then flatten into a round with a rolling pin. Place about 2 tablespoons of filling in the center of each round (place salted egg if desired). Gather and pleat the edges around the top, sealing and pinching to close. Place on individual parchment squares.
