Bangkok Fries

Dare to try these Bangkok Fries!

By Tastemade Culinary Team

Updated: 9/27/2024

Prep Time: 20 min
Cook Time: 45 min
Servings: 4
Easy
 :

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons jasmine rice
  • 1 tablespoon peanut oil
  • 10 1/2 ounces pork mince
  • 1 stalk lemongrass, white part only, thinly sliced diagonally
  • 1 long fresh red chilli, seeded, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, crushed
  • 2 fluid ounces sweet chilli sauce
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • 2 teaspoons brown sugar
  • 1 red onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 large handful coriander leaves
  • 4 kaffir lime leaves, finely shredded
  • 17 3/4 ounces potatoes, cut into chips
  • Sriracha
  • Hoisin
  • Roasted peanut
  • Coriander

Steps

  1. Heat a wok over high heat. Add the rice and cook, tossing, for 3–5 minutes or until lightly golden brown. Remove from heat and set aside to cool slightly. Transfer to a mortar and gently pound with a pestle until crushed. (Alternatively, place in the bowl of a food processor and process until finely chopped.)
  2. Heat the oil in wok over high heat until smoking. Add the mince and cook, stirring to break up any lumps, for 5 minutes or until it starts to brown. Add the lemongrass, chilli and garlic and cook, tossing, for 2 minutes, then remove from the heat. Add the sweet chilli sauce, fish sauce, lime juice and sugar and stir to combine. Set aside for 10 minutes to cool slightly.
