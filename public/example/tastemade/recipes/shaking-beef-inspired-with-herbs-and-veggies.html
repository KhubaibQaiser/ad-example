Shaking Beef Inspired Dish

A fresh take on the Vietnamese dish, Shaking Beef.

By Tastemade Culinary Team

Updated: 9/28/2024

Prep Time: 40 min
Cook Time: 45 min
Servings: 2
Easy
Unit conversion unavailable

Ingredients

  • 1 NY strip or ribeye, sliced 1/4 inch thick
  • 5 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 Thai chili, minced
  • 1 cup Holland House Red Cooking Wine
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1/4 cup sweet soy
  • 2 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 1/2 red onion, julienne
  • 1/2-pound cherry tomato, halved
  • Cilantro for garnish
  • 3/4-pound Persian or mini cucumber, sliced in to 1/4-inch rounds
  • 3/4 cup seasoned rice wine vinegar
  • 1/2 cup water

Steps

  1. Add garlic, Thai chili and Holland House Red Cooking Wine to small pot. Bring to boil and reduce by half. Add in pepper, sweet soy and fish sauce and cook for another minute to incorporate flavors. Chill marinade to room temp.
  2. Meanwhile, prepare the veggies.
  3. For pickled cucumbers, mix liquids and pour over cucumber. Let lightly pickle for about 10 minutes or so.
  4. Once marinade is cool, marinate the beef for 15 minutes. Heat large saut√© pan or wok over high heat. Add in splash of oil. May need to work in two batches, depending on size of pan. Add in beef and stir fry to get caramelization but not overcook. Once almost done, add in onions and tomato and lightly cook.
  5. Transfer to service vessel. Top with cucumbers and cilantro. Great served with steamed jasmine rice.
