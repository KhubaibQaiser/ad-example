Modeling Chocolate

For beginners or advanced pastry chefs, modeling chocolate will be your new favorite tool to achieve picture perfect creations!

By Bernadette Gee

Updated: 9/29/2024

Recipe from:

The Unruly Baker

Prep Time: 20 min
Cook Time: 20 min
Servings: 1
Easy
 :
Nutrition information unavailable

Ingredients

  • 600 grams compound white chocolate
  • 50 grams powdered sugar sifted
  • 105 grams glucose syrup warmed
  • 10 grams cornstarch sifted
  • 30 grams warm water
  • 10–20 grams food colour

Steps

  1. Combine your icing sugar, water, corn starch and liquid glucose in a bowl. Mix well to combine. (If you are making white modeling chocolate you need 20 grams of powdered white food colour which is also called “titanium dioxide”. You can also use white gel food colour White modeling chocolate is the most universal colour to make as it can be coloured any colour after it is set using gel colours. If using, add the food colour to the wet mixture and stir well to combine, until the colour has dissolved and the mixture is uniform and consistent.)
  2. Weigh your white compound chocolate into a microwave safe bowl, gently melt the white compound chocolate in your microwave for a 30 second burst, remove from the microwave and stir then repeat until all the chocolate is melted.
