Fritos® Flavor Twists® Honey BBQ Shrimp & Grits

Add an unexpected twist of flavor to classic shrimp and grits with Honey BBQ Fritos.

By Tastemade Culinary Team

Updated: 9/29/2024

Prep Time: 20 min
Cook Time: 25 min
Servings: 6
Easy
Unit conversion and nutrition information unavailable

Ingredients

Shrimp

  • 1 pound large shrimp
  • Salt and pepper
  • 1 1/2 cups flour
  • 1 bag Fritos® Flavor Twists® Honey BBQ Flavored
  • 3 eggs, beaten
  • Cooking oil spray

Grits

  • 3 1/2 cups chicken stock
  • Salt and pepper
  • 1 cup finely ground cornmeal
  • 1/4 cup butter
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1 cup cheddar cheese, shredded

Creole Sauce

  • 4 pieces of bacon, chopped
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 1 celery stalk, diced
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 2 tablespoon flour
  • 1 can diced fire roasted tomatoes
  • 1/2 cup chicken stock
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1 teaspoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tablespoon creole seasoning
  • 1 tablespoon red wine vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon butter
  • 2 scallions, sliced
  • 1/4 cup chopped parsley leaves
  • Optional: 1-2 tablespoons Louisiana-style hot sauce

Steps

Shrimp

  1. Heat oven to 400 degrees.
  2. Season the shrimp with salt and pepper.
  3. Add the flour, crushed Fritos® Flavor Twists®, and egg to three separate shallow bowls.
  4. Dredge the shrimp in the flour, then egg, and last Fritos® Flavor Twists®, completely coating them.
  5. Place on a parchment lined tray and bake in the oven for 10 minutes.

Grits

  1. Bring 2 1/2 cups chicken stock to a boil.
  2. In a medium bowl combine remaining 1 cup of chicken stock with the cornmeal and stir.
  3. When the chicken stock is boiling, stir in the cornmeal stock mixture.
  4. Turn heat to low and continue to cook, while stirring, for 3-5 more minutes.
  5. Remove from the heat and stir in the butter, cream and cheddar cheese.
  6. Add more stock as needed to loosen grits.

Creole Sauce

  1. Heat a large high sided skillet over medium high heat.
  2. Add the bacon.
  3. Cook until crisp and the fat is rendered.
  4. Remove from the pan to a paper towel lined plate.
  5. Pour most of the bacon fat out of the pan, leaving just enough for sautéing.
  6. Add the onion, celery, garlic and red pepper and season with salt and pepper.
  7. Sauté until soft about 5-7 minutes
  8. Add the flour and cajun seasoning and cook while stirring for 1-2 minutes.
  9. Whisk in the chicken stock, cream, canned tomatoes, Worcestershire sauce, and vinegar and cook just until thickened.
  10. Stir in the cooked bacon and butter.
  11. Serve the shrimp on the grits with sauce and garnish with scallions and parsley.
  12. Top with hot sauce, if using.
